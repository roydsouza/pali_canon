# Vesālī

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[places/INDEX|Places]]

**Vesālī** (Sanskrit: *Vaiśālī*) was the capital of the Vajji confederacy, ruled by the Licchavi clan. It was a prosperous and democratic republic, which the Buddha praised for its unity and public assemblies.

---

## Key Monasteries & Sites

*   **Kūtāgārasālā (Gabled Hall)**: Located in the Mahāvana (Great Forest), this was the primary monastery where the Buddha stayed.
*   **Ambapālivana**: A mango grove gifted to the Sangha by the famous courtesan Ambapālī.
*   **Vālikārāma**: The site of the **Second Buddhist Council** held 100 years after the Buddha's Parinibbāna to resolve a dispute over ten monastic practices.

---

## Historical & Doctrinal Events

*   **Establishment of the Bhikkhunī Sangha**: It was at Vesālī that Mahāpajāpatī Gotamī and 500 Sakyan women arrived with bleeding feet, and through the intercession of [[people/ananda|Ānanda]], the Buddha granted women permission to ordain.
*   **The Last Rains Retreat**: The Buddha spent his final rains retreat (*vassa*) at the nearby village of Beluvagāma, where he fell gravely ill but suppressed the illness through his resolve.
*   **Relinquishing Life Span**: At the Cāpāla Shrine in Vesālī, the Buddha formally announced to Māra that he would pass away in three months.

---

## Key Discourses (Suttas)

*   **[[mula/sutta/digha_nikaya/dn16|DN 16: Mahāparinibbānasutta]]** — The first chapter recounts the Buddha's final journey starting from Rājagaha through Vesālī, teaching the Licchavis the seven conditions for a nation's non-decline.
