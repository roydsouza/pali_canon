# Sāvatthī

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[places/INDEX|Places]]

**Sāvatthī** (Sanskrit: *Śrāvastī*) was the capital of the Kosala kingdom, ruled by King Pasenadi. It was the most important center of early Buddhism, where the Buddha spent twenty-five annual rains retreats (*vassa*) and delivered the majority of his discourses.

---

## Key Monasteries & Sites

*   **Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍikassa Ārāma**: The Jetā Grove, purchased by the wealthy merchant Anāthapiṇḍika from Prince Jeta for a layer of gold coins. It was the Buddha's primary residence.
*   **Pubbārāma (Eastern Monastery)**: Built by the devouress Visākhā, Migāra's mother, a grand two-story stone mansion (*pāsāda*) where the Buddha frequently stayed.

---

## Notable Inhabitants & Interlocutors

*   **King Pasenadi** — The ruler of Kosala, who engaged in numerous dialogues with the Buddha regarding statecraft, aging, and virtue.
*   **Anāthapiṇḍika** — The chief lay patron who established Jetavana.
*   **Visākhā** — The chief female lay patron.
*   **[[people/ananda|Ānanda]]** — Stayed here to attend on the Buddha, hearing and memorizing the suttas.

---

## Doctrinal Significance

Because the Buddha resided here for so many years, it is the setting for major suttas like the **Ānāpānasatisutta (MN 118)**, the **Satipaṭṭhānasutta (MN 10)** commentaries, and numerous numerical discourses.
