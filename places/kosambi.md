# Kosambī

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[places/INDEX|Places]]

**Kosambī** (Sanskrit: *Kauśāmbī*) was the capital of the Vatsa (Vaṃsa) kingdom, situated on the banks of the Yamunā river, ruled by King Udena. It was an important commercial hub.

---

## Key Monasteries & Sites

*   **Ghositārāma**: A park and monastery built by the merchant Ghosita. It was the primary place where the Buddha and his disciples stayed when in Kosambī.
*   **Badarikārāma** and **Kukkuṭārāma**: Other parks established by merchants for the Sangha.

---

## The Monastic Dispute of Kosambī

Kosambī is famous as the site of the first major threat of schism in the Sangha, caused by a minor dispute between a Vinaya expert and a Sutta expert over a ladle of water left in a washroom pot. The dispute escalated, dividing the local monks. The Buddha tried to mediate, but the monks were stubborn, saying: *"Let the Blessed One remain at ease, we will settle this ourselves."*

In response, the Buddha left them and spent the rains retreat alone in the Parileyyaka forest, attended only by an elephant and a monkey. Realizing their error and starved of lay support (as the lay devotees refused to feed quarreling monks), the Kosambī monks repented and went to Sāvatthī to seek the Buddha's forgiveness.

## Notable Interlocutors

*   **[[people/ananda|Ānanda]]** — Visited and taught here, including the dialogue with the Brahmin [[people/unnabha|Uṇṇābha]].
*   **Sāmāvatī** — One of King Udena's queens, a devoted disciple of Khujjuttarā (who memorized the *Itivuttaka*).
