# Rājagaha

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[places/INDEX|Places]]

**Rājagaha** (meaning "House of the King"; Sanskrit: *Rājagṛha*, modern Rajgir) was the capital of the Magadha kingdom, ruled initially by King Bimbisāra and later by his son Ajātasattu. It is surrounded by five hills that provided natural fortification.

---

## Key Monasteries & Sites

*   **Veḷuvana (The Bamboo Grove)**: The first monastery (*ārāma*) accepted by the Buddha, gifted by King Bimbisāra.
*   **Gijjhakūṭa (Vulture Peak)**: A rocky hill where the Buddha spent many retreats and delivered several profound discourses. It was Devadatta's attempt site to kill the Buddha by rolling a boulder down the hill.
*   **Sattapaṇṇī Cave**: Located on one of the hills, this was the site of the **First Buddhist Council** presided over by [[people/mahakassapa|Mahākassapa]] to compile the scriptures.

---

## Notable Inhabitants & Interlocutors

*   **King Bimbisāra** — Devoted lay disciple who patronized the Sangha.
*   **King Ajātasattu** — Who usurped his father Bimbisāra but later repented and became a major patron of the First Council.
*   **[[people/sariputta|Sāriputta]] & [[people/mahamoggallana|Mahāmoggallāna]]** — Ordained here and attained their final parinibbāna in Magadha.
*   **Jīvaka Komārabhacca** — The royal physician who built a monastery in his mango grove for the Buddha.

---

## Doctrinal Significance

Setting for the **Sāmaññaphalasutta (DN 2)**, in which King Ajātasattu questions the Buddha on the visible fruits of the contemplative life.
