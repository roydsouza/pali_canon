### Tisarana (Refuges)
Buddhaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi. Dhammaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi. Saṅghaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi. :: I go to the Buddha for refuge. I go to the Dhamma for refuge. I go to the Saṅgha for refuge.

### Dhammapada 1
Manopubbaṅgamā dhammā, manoseṭṭhā manomayā; :: Mind precedes all mental states, mind is their chief, they are mind-made.
Manasā ce paduṭṭhena, bhāsati vā karoti vā; :: If with an impure mind one speaks or acts,
Tato naṃ dukkhamanveti, cakkaṃva vahato padaṃ. :: suffering follows one, even as the wheel follows the hoof of the ox.

### Dhammapada 183
Sabbapāpassa akaraṇaṃ, kusalassa upasampadā; :: Not to do any evil, to cultivate wholesome action,
Sacittapariyodapanaṃ, etaṃ buddhāna sāsanaṃ. :: to purify one's own mind—this is the teaching of the Buddhas.

### AN 11.15 / Snp 1.8 (Mettā Sutta snippet)
Sukhino va khemino hontu, sabbasantupaddavā; :: May all beings be happy and secure;
Sabbe sattā bhavantu sukhitattā. :: may all beings be happy-hearted.

### MN 19 (Dvedhāvitakka snippet)
Yaññadeva, bhikkhave, bhikkhu bahulamanuvitakketi anuvicāreti :: Mendicants, whatever a mendicant frequently thinks about and ponders,
tathā tathā nati hoti cetaso. :: that becomes the inclination of their mind.

## Memorization Strategy

1. **Aural Familiarity**: Listen to high-quality recordings of the chants (e.g., from Amaravati or Bhante Sujato) to internalize the correct Pali cadence and pronunciation.
2. **Interleaved Recall**: Do not repeat one verse 50 times sequentially. Instead, read it once, close your eyes and recall it, then move to another verse, and come back.
3. **Daily Recitation**: Recite the *Tisarana* and your active verses at the start of every meditation session to compose the mind.
