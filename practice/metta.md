# Mettā (Loving-Kindness Meditation)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault Index]]

---

## Description

**Mettā** (Loving-kindness or goodwill) is the deliberate cultivation of friendly, benevolent feelings toward all living beings. It is the first of the four **Brahmavihāras** (sublime states or divine abodes), alongside compassion (*karuṇā*), appreciative joy (*muditā*), and equanimity (*upekkhā*). Mettā is both a concentration practice (*samatha*) that leads to deep absorption (specifically up to the third jhāna) and an active emotional purification method that serves as the direct antidote to anger, ill will (*vyāpāda*), and aversion.

The practice of Mettā involves developing a warm, friendly, and non-attached attitude, starting with oneself and expanding systematically:
1. **Self**: Establishing goodwill toward oneself as a foundation ("May I be happy, may I be free from suffering").
2. **Benefactor**: Directing goodwill toward a teacher, mentor, or respected person.
3. **Friend**: Directing goodwill toward a dear friend.
4. **Neutral Person**: Directing goodwill toward someone for whom one has no strong positive or negative feelings.
5. **Difficult Person**: Directing goodwill toward someone with whom one has conflict or aversion.
6. **All Beings**: Radiating goodwill in all directions (north, south, east, west, above, below, all around) to all sentient beings without limit or discrimination.

In the suttas, Mettā is often described as a release of the heart (*mettācetovimutti*). When practiced deeply, it breaks down the barriers between self and other, culminating in an immeasurable state of mind that cannot be shaken by anger or hate.

## Sutta References

The following suttas and sections in the vault describe, analyze, or detail the practice of Mettā:

*   [[an11_15|AN 11.15: Karaṇīyamettāsutta]] — The canonical discourse detailing how to live in harmony and cultivate a boundless heart of loving-kindness toward all creatures, just as a mother protects her only child.
*   [[dn13|DN 13: Tevijjasutta]] — Explains the systematic radiation of the four Brahmavihāras (loving-kindness, compassion, appreciative joy, equanimity) in all directions as the path to union with Brahma.
*   [[mn7|MN 7: Vatthūpamasutta]] — Describes the purification of the mind's defilements and the subsequent emergence of joy, rapture, calm, and the practice of the Brahmavihāras.
*   [[an10_60|AN 10.60: Girimānandasutta]] — Lists the ten contemplations, including the development of loving-kindness as an antidote to aversion.
