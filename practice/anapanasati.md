# Ānāpānasati (Mindfulness of Breathing)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault Index]]

---

## Description

**Ānāpānasati** (Mindfulness of breathing) is one of the most widely taught and practiced meditation methods in the Buddhist tradition. In the suttas, it is praised by the Buddha as a practice that, when developed and cultivated, fulfills the **Four Foundations of Mindfulness** (*cattāro satipaṭṭhānā*) and subsequently fulfills the **Seven Awakening Factors** (*satta bojjhaṅgā*), leading to final knowledge and release.

The practice is structured around sixteen sequential steps divided into four tetrads, corresponding to the four satipaṭṭhānā:
1. **First Tetrad (Body - Kāyānupassanā)**:
   * Breathing in long or short, one knows it.
   * Experiencing the whole body while breathing in/out.
   * Calming the bodily fabrication (*kāyasaṅkhāra*) while breathing in/out.
2. **Second Tetrad (Feelings - Vedanānupassanā)**:
   * Experiencing rapture (*pīti*) while breathing in/out.
   * Experiencing pleasure (*sukha*) while breathing in/out.
   * Experiencing the mental fabrication (*cittasaṅkhāra* — feeling/perception) while breathing in/out.
   * Calming the mental fabrication while breathing in/out.
3. **Third Tetrad (Mind - Cittānupassanā)**:
   * Experiencing the state of the mind (*citta*) while breathing in/out.
   * Gladdening the mind while breathing in/out.
   * Stilling/concentrating the mind while breathing in/out.
   * Releasing the mind while breathing in/out.
4. **Fourth Tetrad (Principles - Dhammānupassanā)**:
   * Contemplating impermanence (*anicca*) while breathing in/out.
   * Contemplating fading away (*virāga*) while breathing in/out.
   * Contemplating cessation (*nirodha*) while breathing in/out.
   * Contemplating relinquishment (*paṭinissagga*) while breathing in/out.

By anchoring awareness firmly on the breath, the mind naturally settles, drops the hindrances, and develops deep concentration and insight concurrently.

## Sutta References

The following suttas and sections in the vault describe, analyze, or detail the practice of Ānāpānasati:

*   [[mn118|MN 118: Ānāpānasatisutta]] — The definitive discourse explaining how mindfulness of breathing fulfills the four foundations of mindfulness and the seven factors of awakening.
*   [[mn10|MN 10: Satipaṭṭhānasutta]] (and [[dn22|DN 22: Mahāsatipaṭṭhānasutta]]) — Lists mindfulness of breathing as the opening exercise for the contemplation of the body.
*   [[an10_60|AN 10.60: Girimānandasutta]] — Describes mindfulness of breathing as the tenth and final contemplation taught to heal the monk Girimānanda.
*   [[an8_63|AN 8.63: Saṅkhittasutta]] — Instructs on developing concentration based on the breath as a foundation for daily practice.
