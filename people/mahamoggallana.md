# Mahāmoggallāna Thera

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[people/INDEX|People]]

**Mahāmoggallāna** (also known as Kolita) was the second of the Buddha's two chief disciples (*aggasāvaka*), alongside [[people/sariputta|Sāriputta]]. He was declared by the Buddha to be the foremost in psychic power (*iddhi*).

---

## Biography & Legacy

*   **Ordination**: Born in a village near [[places/rajagaha|Rājagaha]], he went forth with Sāriputta. After ordaining under the Buddha, he practiced meditation in the village of Kallavālamutta. Overcome by sleepiness, the Buddha visited him and instructed him on overcoming drowsiness (AN 7.62). Seven days later, he attained Arhathood.
*   **Role**: He used his spiritual powers to investigate other realms (heavens and hells) and report back to lay followers, showing them the direct results of kamma. He also vanquished powerful adversaries like the dragon Nandopananda.
*   **Passing**: He was beat to death by bandits hired by sectarian rivals, a result of a past-life kamma of neglecting and abusing his parents. Sāriputta passed away shortly before him.

---

## Associated Places

*   [[places/rajagaha|Rājagaha]] — The capital city near where he lived, practiced, and met his final death.

---

## Key Discourses (Suttas)

*   **[[mula/sutta/samyutta_nikaya/INDEX|SN 35.247: Chappāṇakasutta]]** — The Simile of the Six Animals, demonstrating how sensory restraint anchors the mind.
*   **[[mula/sutta/khuddaka_nikaya/theragatha/INDEX|THAG 20.1]]** — Verses of Mahāmoggallāna in the Theragāthā.
*   **[[mula/sutta/anguttara_nikaya/INDEX|AN 7.62: Pacalāyasutta]]** — The Buddha teaches Mahāmoggallāna eight methods to overcome sleepiness during meditation.

---

## Similes Used

*   **The Six Animals** (in SN 35.247): Six different animals (a dog, jackal, crocodile, bird, hyena, monkey) are tied to a strong rope and pull in different directions towards their respective domains. Only when the rope is tied to a firm post (*thambha*) do they become exhausted and lie down. The post represents mindfulness of the body (*kāyagatāsati*).
