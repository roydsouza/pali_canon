# Uṇṇābha Brāhmaṇa

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[people/INDEX|People]]

**Uṇṇābha** was a Brahmin householder who visited Venerable [[people/ananda|Ānanda]] at the Ghositārāma monastery in [[places/kosambi|Kosambī]] to clarify the purpose and limit of the holy life (*brahmacariya*).

---

## The Dialogue (SN 51.15)

In **SN 51.15 (Uṇṇābhabrāhmaṇasutta)**, Uṇṇābha poses a deep philosophical question regarding the path:
*   **Question**: What is the goal of the holy life?
*   **Answer**: Ānanda explains that the goal is the abandoning of desire (*chanda-pahāna*).
*   **Objection**: Uṇṇābha objects that if desire is abandoned by means of desire (i.e., using the desire to practice to end all desire), it would be an endless progression (*chando chandassa pahānāya... na ca tadalantaraṃ*). It seems like trying to end desire with more desire is contradictory.
*   **Resolution**: Ānanda resolves this with the famous **Simile of the Park**. If a person wants to go to a park, they first generate the desire (*chanda*), put forth energy (*viriya*), make a mental resolution (*citta*), and use investigation (*vīmaṃsā*). Once they arrive at the park, the desire, energy, resolve, and investigation are stilled. Similarly, an Arahant uses the four bases of spiritual power (*iddhipāda* — desire, energy, mind, investigation) to reach Arhathood. Once the goal is reached, these path factors are stilled, and there is no more desire for attainment.

Hearing this explanation, Uṇṇābha is filled with joy, declares himself a lay follower, and praises Ānanda's teaching.
