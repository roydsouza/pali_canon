# Mahākassapa Thera

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[people/INDEX|People]]

**Mahākassapa** (born Pipphali Kassapa) was one of the most revered elders in the early Sangha, declared by the Buddha to be the foremost in ascetic practices (*dhutaṅga*).

---

## Biography & Legacy

*   **Ascetic Lifestyle**: He wore rag-robes, lived in the forest, and went for alms without exception. He was so respected that the Buddha once exchanged his own worn-out outer robe for Kassapa's rag-robe as a mark of great honor.
*   **The First Council**: Following the Buddha's passing, Mahākassapa convened and presided over the First Buddhist Council at the Sattapaṇṇī Cave in [[places/rajagaha|Rājagaha]], selecting 500 Arahants to preserve the Dhamma and Vinaya.
*   **Inheritor of the Dharma**: In later Zen/Chan traditions, Mahākassapa is regarded as the first patriarch, having received the silent "flower sermon" transmission from the Buddha.

---

## Associated Places

*   [[places/rajagaha|Rājagaha]] — Location of the Sattapaṇṇī Cave where he led the First Buddhist Council.

---

## Key Discourses (Suttas)

*   **[[mula/sutta/samyutta_nikaya/INDEX|SN 16: Kassapasaṃyutta]]** — The Connected Discourses on Kassapa, detailing his conversations with the Buddha regarding the decline of the Dhamma and the value of ascetic practices.
*   **[[mula/sutta/khuddaka_nikaya/theragatha/INDEX|THAG 18.1]]** — Verses of Mahākassapa in the Theragāthā, describing the joy of dwelling in the forest.
