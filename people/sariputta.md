# Sāriputta Thera

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[people/INDEX|People]]

**Sāriputta** (meaning "son of Sārī") was one of the two chief disciples (*aggasāvaka*) of the Buddha, alongside [[people/mahamoggallana|Mahāmoggallāna]]. He was declared by the Buddha to be the foremost in wisdom (*paññā*).

---

## Biography & Legacy

*   **Ordination**: Born as Upatissa in a village near [[places/rajagaha|Rājagaha]], he was a close friend of Kolita (later Mahāmoggallāna). They initially ordained under Sañjaya Belaṭṭhiputta. Upon hearing the verse of Assaji about dependent origination (*“Ye dhammā hetuppabhavā...”*), Sāriputta immediately attained stream-entry and went with Mahāmoggallāna to ordain under the Buddha.
*   **Abhidhamma Compiler**: According to tradition, the Buddha taught the Abhidhamma to the devas in the Tāvatiṃsa heaven and then summarized it daily for Sāriputta, who systematized and taught it to his disciples, making him the father of the Abhidhamma lineage.
*   **Sāsanadāyāda**: Known for his kindness and teaching ability, he was compared by the Buddha to a mother who gives birth, while Mahāmoggallāna was like a wet-nurse who nurtures.

---

## Associated Places

*   [[places/rajagaha|Rājagaha]] — Place of birth, ordination, and final passing (*parinibbāna*).
*   [[places/savatthi|Sāvatthī]] — Frequent residence in Jetavana, where he taught many key discourses.

---

## Key Discourses (Suttas)

*   **[[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn28|MN 28: Mahāhatthipadopamasutta]]** — The Great Discourse on the Simile of the Elephant's Footprint. Sāriputta teaches the four noble truths through the four physical elements.
*   **MN 141: Saccavibhaṅgasutta** (Not migrated) — Sāriputta delivers a detailed exposition of the Four Noble Truths.
*   **[[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn43|MN 43: Mahāvedallasutta]]** — A series of questions and answers between Sāriputta and Mahākoṭṭhita on cognitive categories, wisdom, and right view.
*   **[[mula/sutta/samyutta_nikaya/INDEX|SN 12.67: Naḷakalāpīsutta]]** — The famous "sheaves of reeds" simile, illustrating the reciprocal conditioning between consciousness (*viññāṇa*) and name-and-form (*nāmarūpa*).
*   **[[mula/sutta/khuddaka_nikaya/theragatha/INDEX|THAG 17.2]]** — Verses of Sāriputta in the Theragāthā.

---

## Similes Used

*   **The Elephant's Footprint** (in MN 28): Just as the footprint of any walking animal fits inside the footprint of an elephant, so too do all wholesome states fit within the Four Noble Truths.
*   **The Sheaves of Reeds** (in SN 12.67): Just as two sheaves of reeds stand leaning against one another, so too do consciousness and name-and-form lean on one another.
