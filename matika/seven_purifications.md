# Seven Purifications (Satta Visuddhi)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[matika/INDEX|Mātika]]

The Seven Purifications (*satta visuddhi*) describe the stage-by-stage progression of ethical and mental purification that leads to the eradication of defilements and final liberation (Arahantship). They serve as the structural framework for the classical Theravada commentarial text, the *Visuddhimagga* (The Path of Purification).

---

## 1. The List

1. **Sīla-visuddhi**  
   *Purification of conduct (virtue / ethics)*
2. **Citta-visuddhi**  
   *Purification of mind (concentration / absorption)*
3. **Diṭṭhi-visuddhi**  
   *Purification of view (correct understanding of mind and body)*
4. **Kaṅkhāvitaraṇa-visuddhi**  
   *Purification by overcoming doubt (discerning the causal relationships of mind and body)*
5. **Maggāmagga-ñāṇadassana-visuddhi**  
   *Purification by knowledge and vision of what is path and what is not path*
6. **Paṭipadā-ñāṇadassana-visuddhi**  
   *Purification by knowledge and vision of the course of practice (the path of insight)*
7. **Ñāṇadassana-visuddhi**  
   *Purification by knowledge and vision (supramundane realization)*

---

## 2. Visuddhimagga Insight Knowledges (*Vipassanā-ñāṇa*) Mapping

In the Theravada scholastic tradition, the first two purifications are the roots of insight (*vipassanā-mūla*). The remaining five purifications correspond to the development of the sixteen insight knowledges (*soḷasa ñāṇa*):

* **Sīla-visuddhi** and **Citta-visuddhi**: Foundations of practice (access and absorption jhānas).
* **Diṭṭhi-visuddhi** (3):
  * **1. Nāmarūpa-pariccheda-ñāṇa**: *Discerning mentality and materiality.*
* **Kaṅkhāvitaraṇa-visuddhi** (4):
  * **2. Paccaya-pariggaha-ñāṇa**: *Discerning causes and conditions.*
* **Maggāmagga-ñāṇadassana-visuddhi** (5):
  * **3. Sammasana-ñāṇa**: *Comprehension (examining formations under the Three Marks).*
  * **4. Udayabbaya-ñāṇa (tender)**: *Arising and passing away (initial phase, overcoming the ten imperfections of insight).*
* **Paṭipadā-ñāṇadassana-visuddhi** (6):
  * **4. Udayabbaya-ñāṇa (mature)**: *Arising and passing away (clear discernment of rise and fall without imperfections).*
  * **5. Bhaṅga-ñāṇa**: *Dissolution (focusing on the passing away of formations).*
  * **6. Bhaya-ñāṇa**: *Appearance as terror (fearing the instability of all conditioned states).*
  * **7. Ādīnava-ñāṇa**: *Danger / disadvantage (seeing all conditioned states as inherently flawed).*
  * **8. Nibbidā-ñāṇa**: *Disenchantment / dispassion.*
  * **9. Muñcitukamyatā-ñāṇa**: *Desire for deliverance (the wish to escape conditioned existence).*
  * **10. Paṭisaṅkhā-ñāṇa**: *Reflective contemplation (re-evaluating formations with determination).*
  * **11. Saṅkhārupekkhā-ñāṇa**: *Equanimity about formations (deep mental poise toward all conditioned things).*
  * **12. Anuloma-ñāṇa**: *Conformity (final adaptation to the path).*
* **Ñāṇadassana-visuddhi** (7):
  * **13. Gotrabhū-ñāṇa**: *Change-of-lineage (crossing the boundary from noble to supramundane).*
  * **14. Magga-ñāṇa**: *Path knowledge (eradicating specific fetters).*
  * **15. Phala-ñāṇa**: *Fruition knowledge (experiencing the peace of Nibbāna).*
  * **16. Paccavekkhaṇa-ñāṇa**: *Reviewing knowledge (inspecting abandoned fetters).*

## Canonical References
* **MN 24: Rathavinīta Sutta** (The Relay Chariots) — The primary source for the seven purifications. Sāriputta and Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta compare the path to a series of seven chariots, each taking the traveller further, showing that each purification is merely a step toward final extinguishment (*anupādā parinibbāna*). (Sutta not yet migrated).
* [[dn2|DN 2: Sāmaññaphala Sutta]] — outlines the gradual training of the monk from ethical conduct (*sīla*), concentration through the jhānas (*citta*), and the development of insight knowledge (*ñāṇa*), aligning with the progression of the purifications.
* [[mn10|MN 10: Satipaṭṭhānasutta]] (and [[dn22|DN 22: Mahāsatipaṭṭhānasutta]]) — the opening declaration declares the four foundations of mindfulness to be the path for the purification of beings (*sattānaṃ visuddhiyā*).
