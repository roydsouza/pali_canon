# Six Recollections (Cha Anussati)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[matika/INDEX|Mātika]]

## The List

1. **Buddhānussati** (Recollection of the Buddha)  
   *Recollecting the qualities of the Tathāgata: accomplished, fully awakened, perfect in knowledge and conduct, sublime, knower of worlds, unsurpassed guide for those who can be trained, teacher of gods and humans, awakened, blessed.*
2. **Dhammānussati** (Recollection of the Dhamma)  
   *Recollecting the qualities of the Teaching: well-expounded by the Blessed One, visible here and now, timeless, inviting inspection, leading onward, to be personally realized by the wise.*
3. **Saṅghānussati** (Recollection of the Saṅgha)  
   *Recollecting the qualities of the Community of disciples: practicing well, practicing uprightly, practicing systematically, practicing dutifully, worthy of gifts, hospitality, offerings, and respect, the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.*
4. **Sīlānussati** (Recollection of Virtue)  
   *Recollecting one's own ethical conduct as untorn, unbroken, unspotted, unsplattered, free, praised by the wise, unclung to, leading directly to concentration.*
5. **Cāgānussati** (Recollection of Generosity)  
   *Recollecting one's own acts of giving: "It is a gain for me, that in a world obsessed by stinginess, I live with a heart free of stinginess, open-handed, finding joy in letting go, committed to charity, enjoying giving and sharing."*
6. **Devatānussati** (Recollection of Deities)  
   *Recollecting the qualities of faith, virtue, learning, generosity, and wisdom that led deities to their heavenly rebirth, and recognizing those same qualities in oneself.*
