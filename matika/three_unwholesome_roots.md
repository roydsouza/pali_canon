# Three Unwholesome Roots (Akusalamūla)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[matika/INDEX|Mātika]]

Also known as the Three Poisons or Fires that drive the cycle of existence.

## The List

1. **Lobha**  
   *Greed (attachment / passion)*
2. **Dosa**  
   *Hatred (ill-will / anger / aversion)*
3. **Moha**  
   *Delusion (ignorance / confusion)*

## Related Lists
* Related: [[dependent_origination|Dependent Origination]] (delusion corresponds to the link of ignorance / avijjā; greed maps to craving / taṇhā and clinging / upādāna)
* Related: [[five_hindrances|Five Hindrances]] (greed manifests as sensual desire; hatred manifests as ill-will; delusion underlies doubt and restlessness)
* Related: [[three_marks|Three Marks of Existence]] (unwholesome roots arise from failing to see the impermanent, painful, and non-self nature of reality)

## Canonical References
* [[mn118|MN 118: Ānāpānasati Sutta]] (insight meditation is practiced to eradicate these roots by seeing things as they really are)
