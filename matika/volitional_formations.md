# Volitional Formations (Saṅkhārā)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[INDEX|Mātika]] / [[five_aggregates|Five Aggregates]] / [[dependent_origination|Dependent Origination]]

---

## Canonical Definition

> "Saṅkhārakkhandho: rūpasañcetanā, saddasañcetanā..."
> 
> *“Volition regarding forms, sounds...”*

---

## Detailed Description

Saṅkhārā refers to the active, fabricating forces of the mind. It encompasses intentions, choices, habits, and emotional responses that shape kamma. It is the constructive activity of consciousness.

---

## Abhidhamma Analysis

In the [[mula/abhidhamma/abhidhammatthasangaha|Abhidhammatthasaṅgaha]] (Chapter 2), the aggregate of volitional formations (*Saṅkhārakkhandha*) includes the remaining 50 mental factors (*cetasika*) besides feeling (*vedanā*) and perception (*saññā*). Among these, volition (*cetanā*) is the chief factor responsible for the generation of kamma.
