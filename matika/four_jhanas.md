# Four Jhānas (Cattāri Jhānāni)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[matika/INDEX|Mātika]]

## The List

1. **Paṭhama jhāna** (First Jhāna)  
   *Accompanied by vitakka (directed thought), vicāra (evaluation), pīti (rapture), sukha (pleasure), and ekaggatā (one-pointedness), born of seclusion, with the five hindrances subsided.*
2. **Dutiya jhāna** (Second Jhāna)  
   *Accompanied by internal confidence, unification of mind, pīti (rapture), sukha (pleasure), and ekaggatā, born of concentration (free of vitakka and vicāra).*
3. **Tatiya jhāna** (Third Jhāna)  
   *Accompanied by equanimity, mindfulness, situational awareness, sukha (pleasure), and ekaggatā, free of rapture.*
4. **Catuttha jhāna** (Fourth Jhāna)  
   *Accompanied by upekkhā (equanimity) and sati (mindfulness) purified by equanimity, neither-pain-nor-pleasure, with all mental and physical fluctuations settled.*

## Canonical References
* [[dn2|DN 2: Sāmaññaphalasutta]] — detailed description of the four jhānas using the classic bathing powder, lotus, and white cloth similes.
* [[mn111|MN 111: Anupadasutta]] — Sāriputta's step-by-step insight analysis of the factors of each jhāna and formless attainment.
* [[an9_36|AN 9.36: Jhānasutta]] — outlines how each level of jhāna serves as a basis for ending the taints (āsavas) through insight.
* [[mn52|MN 52: Aṭṭhakanāgarasutta]] — Ananda outlines the paths to liberation using the four jhānas, four brahmavihāras, and first three formless attainments.
* [[mn128|MN 128: Upakkilesasutta]] — describes how the Buddha overcame the subtle mental imperfections (upakkilesas) that obstruct concentration.
* [[an9_34|AN 9.34: Nibbānasukhasutta]] — Sāriputta explains how the cessation of feeling and perception via jhāna progression is Nibbāna.
* [[an9_35|AN 9.35: Gāvīupamāsutta]] — uses the cow simile to warn against attempting higher jhānas before stabilizing the lower ones.
* [[mn8|MN 8: Sallekhasutta]] — clarifies that the jhānas are peaceful abidings (*diṭṭhadhammasukhavihāra*), but not the ultimate self-effacement (*sallekha*) itself.
* [[an8_63|AN 8.63: Saṅkhittasutta]] — provides brief instructions on using jhāna concentration based on mindfulness and sublime states.
