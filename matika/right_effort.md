# Right Effort (Sammāvāyāmo)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[INDEX|Mātika]] / [[noble_eightfold_path|Noble Eightfold Path]]

---

## Canonical Definition

> "Katamo ca, bhikkhave, sammāvāyāmo? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ anuppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati viriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; 
> uppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati viriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; 
> anuppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ uppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati viriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; 
> uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati viriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati—ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, sammāvāyāmo."
> 
> *“And what, bhikkhus, is right effort? Here, a bhikkhu generates desire, exerts himself, arouses energy, directs his mind, and strives:
> 1. To prevent the arising of unarisen evil unwholesome states;
> 2. To abandon arisen evil unwholesome states;
> 3. To bring about the arising of unarisen wholesome states;
> 4. To maintain arisen wholesome states, to prevent their ruin, to increase, cultivate, and fulfill them:
> this, bhikkhus, is called right effort.”*
> — **SN 45.8 (Vibhaṅgasutta)**

---

## Detailed Description

**Right Effort (*Sammāvāyāmo*)** is the engine of the path. It represents the application of energy (*viriya*) guided by wisdom to purify the mind. Right Effort is neither lazy passivity nor straining force; it is the balanced, intelligent application of willpower to cultivate the mind.

Right Effort is systematically structured into the **Four Right Exertions** (*cattāro sammappadhānā*):
1.  **Prevention (*Saṃvara*)**: Guarding the senses to prevent unarisen unwholesome states (such as greed, anger, and distractions) from entering the mind.
2.  **Abandonment (*Pahāna*)**: Actively letting go of and dissolving unwholesome states that have already arisen (such as sensual lust, anger, sluggishness, restlessness, or doubt).
3.  **Development (*Bhāvanā*)**: Cultivating and arising wholesome states (such as mindfulness, tranquility, joy, and wisdom) that have not yet arisen.
4.  **Maintenance (*Anurakkhaṇā*)**: Guarding, strengthening, and bringing to full maturity the wholesome states that are already present in the mind.

In practice, Right Effort involves learning the "tuning" of one's energy. Just as a lute's strings must not be too tight or too loose, mental effort must be balanced between over-exertion (which leads to restlessness) and under-exertion (which leads to sluggishness).
