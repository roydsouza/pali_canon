# Ten Fetters (Dasa Saṃyojanā)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[matika/INDEX|Mātika]]

The Ten Fetters (*dasa saṃyojanā*) are the mental bonds that bind sentient beings to saṃsāra (the cycle of birth, aging, and death). They are systematically severed as a practitioner progresses through the four stages of awakening.

---

## The List

1. **[[identity_view|Sakkāyadiṭṭhi]]** — *Identity view / Self-view*
2. **[[skeptical_doubt|Vicikicchā]]** — *Skeptical doubt / Indecision*
3. **[[clinging_to_rites_and_rituals|Sīlabbataparāmāso]]** — *Clinging to rules and rituals*
4. **[[sensual_desire|Kāmarāgo]]** — *Sensual desire / Lust*
5. **[[ill_will_or_hostility|Paṭigho]]** — *Ill will / Anger / Aversion*
6. **[[lust_for_material_existence|Rūparāgo]]** — *Lust for material existence (Brahma realms)*
7. **[[lust_for_immaterial_existence|Arūparāgo]]** — *Lust for immaterial existence (Formless realms)*
8. **[[conceit|Māno]]** — *Conceit / Pride*
9. **[[restlessness_and_remorse|Uddhaccaṃ]]** — *Restlessness / Agitation*
10. **[[ignorance|Avijjā]]** — *Ignorance / Delusion*

---

## 2. Stages of Awakening Mapping

The fetters are divided into lower and higher groups, which are abandoned progressively at the four stages of noble attainment:

### The Five Lower Fetters (*Orambhāgiyāni Saṃyojanāni*)
These bind beings to the sensual realm (*kāmadhātu*):
* **Stream-entry (Sotāpatti)**: Fully abandons the first three fetters (**1. Sakkāya-diṭṭhi**, **2. Vicikicchā**, and **3. Sīlabbata-parāmāsa**). A stream-enterer is guaranteed full awakening within at most seven lifetimes and can never be reborn in the lower realms.
* **Once-return (Sakadāgāmī)**: Significantly weakens the fourth and fifth fetters (**4. Kāmarāga** and **5. Vyāpāda**).
* **Non-return (Anāgāmī)**: Fully abandons the fourth and fifth fetters (**4. Kāmarāga** and **5. Vyāpāda**). A non-returner is reborn in the Pure Abodes (*Suddhāvāsa*) and attains final extinguishment there.

### The Five Higher Fetters (*Uddhambhāgiyāni Saṃyojanāni*)
These bind beings to the fine-material and formless realms (*rūpa-* and *arūpadhātu*):
* **Arahantship (Arahatta)**: Fully abandons the remaining five fetters (**6. Rūparāga**, **7. Arūparāga**, **8. Māna**, **9. Uddhacca**, and **10. Avijjā**). An Arahant has ended all suffering and will not be reborn.

---

## 3. Abhidhamma / Commentarial List

In the Abhidhamma Piṭaka (e.g., Dhammasaṅgaṇī and Vibhaṅga) and the commentaries (Atthakathā), a slightly modified list is often presented, particularly in the context of the six sense bases:

1. **Kāmarāga-saṃyojana** (*Sensual lust*)
2. **Paṭigha-saṃyojana** (*Aversion / ill-will*)
3. **Māna-saṃyojana** (*Conceit*)
4. **Diṭṭhi-saṃyojana** (*Wrong views*) — *replaces Sakkāya-diṭṭhi and incorporates other speculative views*
5. **Vicikicchā-saṃyojana** (*Sceptical doubt*)
6. **Sīlabbata-parāmāsa-saṃyojana** (*Clinging to rules and rituals*)
7. **Bhavarāga-saṃyojana** (*Lust for existence*) — *combines Rūparāga and Arūparāga*
8. **Issā-saṃyojana** (*Envy / jealousy*) — *added*
9. **Macchariya-saṃyojana** (*Miserliness / avarice*) — *added*
10. **Avijjā-saṃyojana** (*Ignorance*)

## Canonical References
* [[sn55|SN 55: Sotāpattisaṃyutta]] — focuses on the attainment of Stream-entry (*sotāpatti*), which is defined by the complete eradication of the first three fetters.
* [[mn10|MN 10: Satipaṭṭhānasutta]] (and [[dn22|DN 22: Mahāsatipaṭṭhānasutta]]) — in the contemplation of principles (*dhammānupassanā*) on the six sense bases, the meditator understands the eye, forms, and the fetter (*saṃyojana*) that arises dependent on both, and how an unarisen fetter arises, how an arisen fetter is abandoned, and how an abandoned fetter does not arise again.
* [[sn22|SN 22: Khandhasaṃyutta]] — teaches how attachment and identification with the five aggregates act as the bond and fetter that prevents liberation.
* [[sn48|SN 48: Indriyasaṃyutta]] — SN 48.12 links the progressive development of the five spiritual faculties directly to the destruction of the fetters and the stages of awakening.
