# Right Intention (Sammāsaṅkappo)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[INDEX|Mātika]] / [[noble_eightfold_path|Noble Eightfold Path]]

---

## Canonical Definition

> "Katamo ca, bhikkhave, sammāsaṅkappo? Nekkhammasaṅkappo, avyāpādasaṅkappo, avihiṃsāsaṅkappo—ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, sammāsaṅkappo."
> 
> *“And what, bhikkhus, is right intention? The intention of renunciation, the intention of non-ill will, and the intention of harmlessness: this, bhikkhus, is called right intention.”*
> — **SN 45.8 (Vibhaṅgasutta)**

---

## Detailed Description

**Right Intention (*Sammāsaṅkappo*)** represents the emotional and volitional aspect of the path. While Right View provides the cognitive framework, Right Intention translates this understanding into our underlying mental inclinations, purification of thoughts, and resolves.

Right Intention is defined through three wholesome resolves that counter the three unwholesome roots:
1.  **Resolve on Renunciation (*Nekkhammasaṅkappa*)**: The intention to let go of sensual attachments, greed, and possessiveness. Renunciation is not merely ascetic deprivation but the freedom of mind that arises when one sees that sensual pleasures are fleeting, bound up with anxiety, and unable to yield lasting satisfaction. It counters the root of Greed (*Lobha*).
2.  **Resolve on Non-Ill Will (*Avyāpādasaṅkappa*)**: The intention of loving-kindness (*mettā*), wishing for the welfare, happiness, and peace of all living beings. It counters the root of Hatred (*Dosa*) and prevents thoughts of anger, resentment, or hostility from taking root in the mind.
3.  **Resolve on Harmlessness (*Avihiṃsasaṅkappa*)**: The intention of compassion (*karuṇā*), wishing that all beings be free from suffering and pain. It manifests as a deep sensitivity to the suffering of others and a firm commitment to avoid causing harm, directly countering the urge to injure, bully, or destroy.

Like Right View, Right Intention has two levels: mundane (thoughts and wishes aimed at worldly goodness and wholesome rebirth) and noble/supramundane (the resolve of a mind aligned with the path of awakening, free from defilements). Right Intention serves as the bridge between wisdom (*paññā*) and ethical action (*sīla*), guiding speech, action, and livelihood.

## Sutta References

The following suttas in the vault mention, describe, or analyze Right Intention:

*   [[../mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn19|MN 19: Dvedhāvitakkasutta]] — The primary sutta where the Buddha describes his pre-awakening practice of dividing his thoughts into two categories (unwholesome: sensuality, ill will, harm; wholesome: renunciation, non-ill will, harmlessness), illustrating that whatever one frequently ponders becomes the inclination of the mind.
*   [[../mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn117|MN 117: Mahācattārīsakasutta]] — Delineates the difference between mundane intention (accompanied by defilements but yielding merit) and supramundane intention (resolved on cessation, free from attachments), showing how Right View guides it.
*   [[../mula/sutta/digha_nikaya/dn22|DN 22: Mahāsatipaṭṭhānasutta]] — Defines Right Intention under the Truths section of the Dhammānupassanā.
*   [[../mula/sutta/samyutta_nikaya/INDEX|SN 45.8: Vibhaṅgasutta]] — The primary path analysis defining each of the eight path factors.
