# Five Spiritual Faculties (Pañcindriya)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[matika/INDEX|Mātika]]

These five qualities are also referred to as the Five Powers (*Pañcabala*) when developed to a degree where they are unshakable.

## The List

1. **Saddhindriya (Saddhā)**  
   *The faculty of faith (conviction)*
2. **Viriyindriya (Viriya)**  
   *The faculty of energy (persistence / effort)*
3. **Satindriya (Sati)**  
   *The faculty of mindfulness*
4. **Samādhindriya (Samādhi)**  
   *The faculty of concentration (unification of mind)*
5. **Paññindriya (Paññā)**  
   *The faculty of wisdom (discernment)*

## Canonical References
* [[sn46|SN 46: Bojjhaṅgasaṃyutta]] — the bojjhaṅgas and the indriyas share the factors of sati, viriya, and samādhi; SN 46.51 on nutriment discusses how these qualities mutually support each other
* [[mn118|MN 118: Ānāpānasatisutta]] — ānāpānasati directly cultivates and fulfills all five spiritual faculties through the progression of the four foundations and seven awakening factors
* [[an4_123_126|AN 4.123–126: Nānākaraṇasuttāni]] — Samādhindriya; the four suttas on differences between meditators examine how the same absorption level leads to varying rebirths depending on the depth of samādhi and paññā
* [[an10_2_6|AN 10.2–6: Ānisaṃsasuttāni]] — the sīla→samādhi→paññā chain in these five benefit-suttas traces the sequential arising of all five spiritual faculties from the basis of saddha (confidence in the teaching)
* [[sn35|SN 35: Saḷāyatanasaṃyutta]] — the six sense bases (āyatanā) as the domain in which the five spiritual faculties are applied to produce dispassion and liberation; the aniccā/dukkha/anattā contemplation of the āyatanā directly exercises paññindriya
* [[sn55|SN 55: Sotāpattisaṃyutta]] — teaches the development of unwavering confidence (aveccappasāda, which is the unshakable form of saddhindriya) in the Triple Gem, which together with ethical behavior constitutes the factors of stream-entry
* [[sn48|SN 48: Indriyasaṃyutta]] — the primary collection on the five spiritual faculties, defining them in detail (SN 48.9-10), outlining their respective practice domains (SN 48.8), and showing how their development determines the stage of awakening (SN 48.12).
* [[udana#Ud 4.1|Ud 4.1: Meghiya Sutta]] — Outlines the five conditions that lead to the maturity of liberation (virtue, spiritual friendship, talk on renunciation, energy/*viriya*, and wisdom/*paññā* into rise and fall), which map directly onto the development of the spiritual faculties.
* [[snp1.4|Snp 1.4: Kasībhāradvājasutta]] — uses the agricultural metaphor where faith (*saddhā*) is the seed, wisdom (*paññā*) is the yoke/plow, and energy (*viriya*) is the draft ox.
* [[snp1.10|Snp 1.10: Āḷavakasutta]] — the Buddha answers how one crosses the flood (by faith/*saddhā*), how one crosses the ocean (by diligence), and how one is purified (by wisdom/*paññā*).
