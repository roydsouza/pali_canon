# Right Livelihood (Sammāājīvo)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[INDEX|Mātika]] / [[noble_eightfold_path|Noble Eightfold Path]]

---

## Canonical Definition

> "Katamo ca, bhikkhave, sammāājīvo? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako micchāājīvaṃ pahāya sammāājīvena jīvikaṃ kappeti—ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, sammāājīvo."
> 
> *“And what, bhikkhus, is right livelihood? Here, bhikkhus, a noble disciple, having abandoned wrong livelihood, earns their living by right livelihood: this, bhikkhus, is called right livelihood.”*
> — **SN 45.8 (Vibhaṅgasutta)**

---

## Detailed Description

**Right Livelihood (*Sammāājīvo*)** extends ethical conduct (*sīla*) to our professional and economic lives. Because a significant portion of our time is spent earning a living, our livelihood has a profound impact on our mind, our values, and the world. 

For **lay practitioners**, Right Livelihood means earning a living honestly, legally, and peacefully, avoiding occupations that cause direct harm or suffering to other sentient beings. In the canonical tradition (specifically in the *Vaṇijjasutta*, AN 5.177), the Buddha identifies **five wrong trades** (*micchā-vaṇijjā*) that a lay follower should not undertake:
1.  **Trading in Weapons (*sattha-vaṇijjā*)**: Manufacturing or selling weapons of war and violence.
2.  **Trading in Living Beings (*satta-vaṇijjā*)**: Human trafficking, slavery, prostitution, or breeding animals for slaughter.
3.  **Trading in Meat (*maṃsa-vaṇijjā*)**: Selling the flesh of slaughtered animals, or raising animals for meat.
4.  **Trading in Intoxicants (*majja-vaṇijjā*)**: Manufacturing or selling alcohol, drugs, or other mind-altering substances.
5.  **Trading in Poisons (*visa-vaṇijjā*)**: Manufacturing or selling toxic chemicals, pesticides, or poisons meant to kill.

For **monastics**, Right Livelihood means living purely off the alms and support of lay supporters, refraining from deceit, flattery, scheming, hinting, belittling, or pursuing gain with gain. It involves guarding the senses and living strictly within the monastic training code (*Vinaya*).
