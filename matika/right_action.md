# Right Action (Sammākammanto)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[INDEX|Mātika]] / [[noble_eightfold_path|Noble Eightfold Path]]

---

## Canonical Definition

> "Katamo ca, bhikkhave, sammākammanto? Pāṇātipātā veramaṇī, adinnādānā veramaṇī, kāmesumicchācārā veramaṇī—ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, sammākammanto."
> 
> *“And what, bhikkhus, is right action? Abstaining from taking life, abstaining from taking what is not given, and abstaining from sexual misconduct: this, bhikkhus, is called right action.”*
> — **SN 45.8 (Vibhaṅgasutta)**

---

## Detailed Description

**Right Action (*Sammākammanto*)** is the physical manifestation of ethical integrity (*sīla*). It translates our inner resolves of non-ill will and harmlessness into physical actions, ensuring that our bodily behavior does not cause suffering to other sentient beings.

Right Action comprises three primary abstinences:
1.  **Abstaining from Taking Life (*Pāṇātipātā veramaṇī*)**: Refraining from killing, injuring, or harming any living being (human, animal, or insect). Instead, one dwells with compassion, sympathy, and care for the welfare of all living creatures.
2.  **Abstaining from Taking What is Not Given (*Adinnādānā veramaṇī*)**: Refraining from stealing, robbery, fraud, or taking others' property without permission. It involves living honestly, respecting the possessions of others, and cultivating generosity.
3.  **Abstaining from Sexual Misconduct (*Kāmesumicchācārā veramaṇī*)**: For lay practitioners, this means refraining from adulterous relationships, sexual exploitation, abuse, or any sexual activity that violates the trust and commitments of others. For monastics, this factor is developed into complete celibacy (*brahmacariya*).

Right Action is not merely a set of restrictive rules; it is a source of freedom. By avoiding harmful actions, we remove the burden of guilt, regret, and fear of consequences, paving the way for the mental stillness required for deep concentration (*samādhi*).
