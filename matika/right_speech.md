# Right Speech (Sammāvācā)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[INDEX|Mātika]] / [[noble_eightfold_path|Noble Eightfold Path]]

---

## Canonical Definition

> "Katamā ca, bhikkhave, sammāvācā? Musāvādā veramaṇī, pisuṇāya vācāya veramaṇī, pharusāya vācāya veramaṇī, samphappalāpā veramaṇī—ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, sammāvācā."
> 
> *“And what, bhikkhus, is right speech? Abstaining from false speech, abstaining from divisive speech, abstaining from harsh speech, and abstaining from idle chatter: this, bhikkhus, is called right speech.”*
> — **SN 45.8 (Vibhaṅgasutta)**

---

## Detailed Description

**Right Speech (*Sammāvācā*)** is the first factor of the ethical training group (*sīla*) on the path. Speech is a primary medium of human interaction and holds immense power to generate wholesome or unwholesome kamma. By training the tongue, we safeguard the mind from anger and greed and cultivate peace in our relationships.

Right Speech consists of refraining from four negative forms of speech:
1.  **Abstaining from False Speech (*Musāvādā veramaṇī*)**: Telling the truth, speaking honestly, and being reliable. A practitioner does not deliberately lie for their own benefit, for others' benefit, or for any reward.
2.  **Abstaining from Divisive Speech (*Pisuṇāya vācāya veramaṇī*)**: Not repeating what one hears to sow discord or split groups. Right speech aims at reconciliation, harmony, and uniting those who are divided.
3.  **Abstaining from Harsh Speech (*Pharusāya vācāya veramaṇī*)**: Avoiding words that are angry, insulting, sarcastic, or abusive. Instead, one speaks words that are gentle, polite, soothing, and reach the heart.
4.  **Abstaining from Idle Chatter (*Samphappalāpā veramaṇī*)**: Avoiding talk that is useless, trivial, or spoken at the wrong time. Right speech is meaningful, timely, reasoned, and connected to the Dhamma.

Right Speech also includes active wholesome speech: speaking what is true, useful, spoken with a mind of goodwill, polite, and timely. In its supramundane level, Right Speech is the abstinence from these four types of speech in a mind that is established on the path of liberation.
