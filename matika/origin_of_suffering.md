# Origin of Suffering (Samudaya)

**Navigation**: [[../INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[INDEX|Mātika]] / [[four_noble_truths|Four Noble Truths]]

---

## Canonical Definition

> "Yāyaṃ taṇhā ponobhavikā nandirāgasahagatā..."
> 
> *“It is this craving which leads to new rebirth, accompanied by delight and lust...”*

---

## Detailed Description

Samudaya refers to the cause of suffering, identified as craving (taṇhā) of three kinds: craving for sensual pleasures (kāma-taṇhā), craving for existence/becoming (bhava-taṇhā), and craving for non-existence (vibhava-taṇhā). This craving keeps beings bound to the wheel of rebirth.
