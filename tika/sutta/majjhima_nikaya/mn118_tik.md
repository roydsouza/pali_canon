# Ānāpānasati Suttavaṇṇanātīkā (Sub-commentary)

### 8. Ānāpānassatisuttavaṇṇanā


### §144

**144. Pubbenāti nissakke karaṇavacanaṃ.**  
*144. "Pubbenā" is an instrumental case used in the sense of ablative separation.*  

**‘‘Aparaṃ visesa’’nti vuttattā visesavisayo ca pubbasaddoti āha – ‘‘sīlaparipūraṇādito pubbavisesato’’ti.**  
*Since the phrase "subsequent distinction" (aparaṃ visesaṃ) is spoken, the word pubba (former) is used in reference to distinction; therefore it is said: "sīlaparipūraṇādito pubbavisesato" (from the former distinction, such as the purification of virtue).*  


### §145

**145. Āraddho yathānusiṭṭhaṃ paṭipattiyā ārādhito.**  
*145. "Āraddho" (satisfied) means pleased by practice in accordance with the instruction.*  

**Yadatthāya sāsane pabbajjā, visesāpatti ca, tadevettha appattanti adhippetaṃ, taṃ jhānavipassanānimittanti āha – ‘‘appattassa arahattassā’’ti.**  
*That for the sake of which there is ordination in the dispensation and the attainment of distinction is what is intended here by "the unattained" (appatta); that is the cause of jhāna and insight, which is why it is said: "appattassa arahattassā" (of the unattained Arahantship).*  

**Komudīti kumudavatī.**  
*"Komudī" means possessing white water-lilies (kumuda).*  

**Tadā kira kumudāni supupphitāni honti.**  
*At that time, indeed, the white water-lilies are in full bloom.*  

**Tenāha – ‘‘kumudānaṃ atthitāya komudī’’ti.**  
*For this reason it is said: "kumudānaṃ atthitāya komudī" (by the presence of kumudas, it is called komudī).*  

**Kumudānaṃ samūho, kumudāni eva vā komudā, te ettha atthīti komudīti.**  
*A multitude of kumudas, or the kumudas themselves, are called komudā, and because they are present in it, it is komudī.*  

**Pavāraṇasaṅgahanti mahāpavāraṇaṃ akatvā āgamanīyasaṅgahaṇaṃ.**  
*"Pavāraṇasaṅgahaṃ" (postponement of the invitation) means gathering the monks who are arriving without holding the great invitation.*  

**Āraddhavipassakassāti ārabhitavipassanassa, vipassanaṃ vaḍḍhetvā ussukkāpetvā vipassissa.**  
*"Āraddhavipassakassa" (of the active meditator of insight) means one who has begun insight, who, having developed and exerted insight, is contemplating.*  

**Bhikkhū idha osarissanti vutthavassā pavāritapavāraṇā ‘‘bhagavantaṃ vandissāma, kammaṭṭhānaṃ sodhessāma, yathāladdhaṃ visesañca pavedissāmā’’ti ajjhāsayena.**  
*"Bhikkhū idha osarissanti" (monks will assemble here) means those who have spent the rains and completed the invitation, with the intention: "We will pay homage to the Blessed One, purify our meditation subjects, and report the distinctions we have respectively obtained."*  

**Ime bhikkhūti ime taruṇasamathavipassanā bhikkhū.**  
*"Ime bhikkhū" (these monks) means these monks whose calm and insight are still immature.*  

**Visesaṃ nibbattetuṃ na sakkhissanti senāsanasappāyādialābhena.**  
*"Visesaṃ nibbattetuṃ na sakkhissanti" (will not be able to produce distinction) because of not obtaining suitable lodgings etc.*  

**Apalibuddhanti aññehi anupaddutaṃ.**  
*"Apalibuddhaṃ" (free of obstacles) means unhindered by others.*  

**Senāsanaṃ gahetuṃ na labhanti antovassabhāvato.**  
*"Senāsanaṃ gahetuṃ na labhanti" (cannot take lodging) because they are still within the rainy season.*  

**Ekassa dinnopi sabbesaṃ dinnoyeva hoti, tasmā sutasutaṭṭhāneyeva ekamāsaṃ vasitvā osariṃsu.**  
*"Ekassa dinnopi sabbesaṃ dinnoyeva hoti" (even if given to one, it is given to all): therefore, they stayed for one month in their respective places and then assembled.*  


### §146

**146. Alanti yuttaṃ, opāyikanti attho, ‘‘alameva nibbinditu’’ntiādīsu (dī. ni. 2.272; saṃ. ni. 2.124, 128, 134, 143) viya.**  
*146. "Alaṃ" means suitable, having the meaning of appropriate, as in "it is indeed suitable to become disenchanted" etc. (Dīgha Nikāya 2.272; Saṃyutta Nikāya 2.124, etc.).*  

**Puṭabaddhaṃ pariharitvā asitaṃ puṭosaṃ a-kārassa o-kāraṃ katvā.**  
*Carrying provisions bound in a bag is called "puṭosa", changing the letter 'a' to 'o'.*  

**Tenāha ‘‘pātheyya’’nti.**  
*For this reason it is said: "pātheyyaṃ" (provisions for a journey).*  


### §147

**147. Vipassanā kathitāti aniccasaññāmukheneva vipassanābhāvanā kathitā.**  
*147. "Vipassanā kathitā" (insight is spoken of) means the development of insight is spoken of through the gateway of the perception of impermanence itself.*  

**Na hi kevalāya aniccānupassanāya vipassanākiccaṃ samijjhati.**  
*Indeed, the function of insight is not accomplished by the contemplation of impermanence alone.*  

**Bahū bhikkhū te ca vitthārarucikāti adhippāyo.**  
*"Bahū bhikkhū" (many monks): and they are of those who delight in detailed exposition—this is the intention.*  

**Tenāha ‘‘tasmā’’tiādi.**  
*For this reason he said: "tasmā" (therefore) and so forth.*  


### §149

**149. Sabbatthāti sabbavāresu.**  
*149. "Sabbatthā" means in all sections.*  

**‘‘Tasmā tiha, bhikkhave, vedanānupassī’’tiādīsupi pītipaṭisaṃveditādivaseneva vedanānupassanāya vuttattā, ‘‘sukhavedanaṃ sandhāyetaṃ vutta’’nti āha.**  
*Since contemplation of feelings is spoken of by way of experiencing rapture etc. in the passage "that is why, monks, at that time a mendicant is contemplating feelings..." and so forth, it is said: "sukhavedanaṃ sandhāyetaṃ vuttaṃ" (this is said in reference to pleasant feeling).*  

**Satipaṭṭhānabhāvanāmanasikāratāya vuttaṃ – ‘‘sādhukaṃ manasikāra’’nti.**  
*Because of the application of mind in developing the foundations of mindfulness, it is called: "sādhukaṃ manasikāraṃ" (careful application of mind).*  

**Saññānāmena paññā vuttā tesaṃ payogattā.**  
*"Saññānāmena paññā vuttā" (wisdom is spoken of by the name of perception) because of their application.*  

**Manasikāranāmena vedanā vuttā, bhāvanāya paricitattā ārammaṇassa manasikāranti katvā.**  
*"Manasikāranāmena vedanā vuttā" (feeling is spoken of by the name of application of mind): having made the object of development familiar, it is called "application of mind."*  

**Vitakkavicāre ṭhapetvāti vuttaṃ vacīsaṅkhārattā tesaṃ.**  
*"Vitakkavicāre ṭhapetvā" (excluding applied and sustained thought) is said because they are verbal processes (vacī-saṅkhāra).*  

**Evaṃ santepīti yadipi manasikārapariyāpannatāya ‘‘manasikāro’’ti vuttaṃ, evaṃ sante vedanānupassanābhāvo na yujjati, assāsapassāsā hissa ārammaṇaṃ.**  
*"Evaṃ santepi" (even if it is so): even though it is called "application of mind" because it is included in application of mind, if this is so, the state of the contemplation of feeling is not applicable, for the in-breaths and out-breaths are its object.*  

**Vatthunti sukhādīnaṃ vedanānaṃ pavattiṭṭhānabhūtaṃ vatthuṃ ārammaṇaṃ katvā vedanāva vediyati, vedanāya ekantabhāvadassanena tassa vedanānupassanābhāvo yujjati evāti imamatthaṃ dasseti.**  
*"Vatthu" refers to the base which is the place of occurrence of feelings like pleasure; "ārammaṇaṃ katvā vedanāva vedayati" (making it the object, feeling itself feels): by showing the exclusive state of feeling, he shows that the state of the contemplation of feeling is indeed applicable.*  

**Etassa anuyogassa.**  
*"Etassa" means of this objection.*  

**Dvīhākārehīti ye sandhāya vuttaṃ, te dassento ‘‘ārammaṇato asammohato cā’’ti āha.**  
*"Dvīhākārehi" (in two ways): showing those in reference to which it is spoken, he said: "ārammaṇato asammohato ca" (by way of the object and by way of non-delusion).*  

**Sappītike dve jhāneti pītisahagatāni paṭhamadutiyajjhānāni paṭipāṭiyā samāpajjati.**  
*"Sappītike dve jhāne" (the two jhānas accompanied by rapture): one enters the first and second jhānas accompanied by rapture in succession.*  

**Samāpattikkhaṇeti samāpajjanakkhaṇe.**  
*"Samāpattikkhaṇe" means at the moment of entering.*  

**Jhānapaṭilābhenāti jhānena samaṅgībhāvena.**  
*"Jhānapaṭilābhena" means by possession of the jhāna.*  

**Ārammaṇato ārammaṇamukhena tadārammaṇajhānapariyāpannā pīti paṭisaṃviditā hoti ārammaṇassa paṭisaṃviditattā.**  
*"Ārammaṇato" (by way of the object): through the gateway of the object, rapture is experienced as included in the jhāna of that object, because the object itself is experienced.*  

**Yathā nāma sappapariyesanaṃ carantena tassa āsaye paṭisaṃvidite sopi paṭisaṃviditova hoti mantāgadabalena tassa gahaṇassa sukarattā; evaṃ pītiyā āsayabhūte ārammaṇe paṭisaṃvidite sā pīti paṭisaṃviditā eva hoti salakkhaṇato sāmaññalakkhaṇato ca tassā gahaṇassa sukarattā.**  
*Just as when a person searching for a snake has experienced its hole, the snake itself is experienced, because its capture is easy by the power of mantras and medicine; in the same way, when the object which is the base of rapture is experienced, that rapture is indeed experienced from its individual characteristic and general characteristic, because its grasp is easy.*  

**Vipassanākkhaṇeti vipassanāpaññāpubbaṅgamāya maggapaññāya visesato dassanakkhaṇe.**  
*"Vipassanākkhaṇe" means at the moment of specific seeing by the path-wisdom preceded by insight-wisdom.*  

**Lakkhaṇapaṭivedhāti pītiyā salakkhaṇassa sāmaññalakkhaṇassa ca paṭivijjhanena.**  
*"Lakkhaṇapaṭivedhā" means by penetrating the individual characteristic and general characteristic of rapture.*  

**Yañhi pītiyā visesato sāmaññato ca lakkhaṇaṃ, tasmiṃ vidite sā yāthāvato viditā hoti. Tenāha – ‘‘asammohato pīti paṭisaṃviditā hotī’’ti.**  
*For when the specific and general characteristics of rapture are known, it is known in reality. For this reason it is said: "asammohato pīti paṭisaṃviditā hoti" (rapture is experienced by way of non-delusion).*  

**Idāni tamatthaṃ pāḷiyā vibhāvetuṃ, ‘‘vuttampi ceta’’ntiādi vuttaṃ.**  
*Now, to clarify this matter from the text, he said "vuttampi cetaṃ" (it is also stated) and so forth.*  

**Tattha dīghaṃ assāsavasenāti dīghassa assāsassa ārammaṇabhūtassa vasena.**  
*There, "dīghaṃ assāsavasena" (by way of a long in-breath) means by way of the long in-breath which is the object.*  

**Cittassa ekaggataṃ avikkhepaṃ pajānatoti jhānapariyāpannaṃ avikkhepoti laddhanāmaṃ cittassekaggataṃ taṃsampayuttāya paññāya pajānato.**  
*"Cittassa ekaggataṃ avikkhepaṃ pajānato" (knowing the one-pointedness of mind and non-distraction) means knowing the one-pointedness of mind, which has received the name "non-distraction," included in the jhāna, by the wisdom associated with it.*  

**Yatheva hi ārammaṇamukhena pīti paṭisaṃviditā hoti, evaṃ taṃsampayuttadhammāpi ārammaṇamukhena paṭisaṃviditā eva honti.**  
*Just as through the gateway of the object, rapture is experienced, in the same way, the states associated with it are also experienced through the gateway of the object.*  

**Sati upaṭṭhitā hotīti dīghaṃ assāsavasena jhānasampayuttā sati tasmiṃ ārammaṇe upaṭṭhite ārammaṇamukhena jhānepi upaṭṭhitā eva nāma hoti.**  
*"Sati upaṭṭhitā hoti" (mindfulness is established) means the mindfulness associated with jhāna by way of the long in-breath is indeed established in the jhāna through the gateway of the object when that object is established.*  

**Tāya satiyāti evaṃ upaṭṭhitāya tāya satiyā yathāvuttena tena ñāṇena suppaṭividitattā ārammaṇassa tassa vasena tadārammaṇā sā pīti paṭisaṃviditā hoti.**  
*"Tāya satiyā" (by that mindfulness): by that mindfulness established in this way, by the aforementioned "tena ñāṇena" (by that knowledge), because the object is well penetrated, by way of that, that rapture is experienced having that object.*  

**Avasesapadānipīti ‘‘dīghaṃ passāsavasenā’’tiādipadānipi.**  
*"Avasesapadānipī" means the steps starting with "by way of a long out-breath" as well.*  

**Evaṃ paṭisambhidāmagge vuttamatthaṃ imasmiṃ sutte耀jetvā dassetuṃ, ‘‘itī’’tiādi vuttaṃ.**  
*Thus, to connect and show the meaning stated in the Paṭisambhidāmagga in this sutta, he said "iti" and so forth.*  

**Imināpi yoginā manasikārena paṭilabhitabbato paṭilābhoti vuttaṃ – ‘‘jhānasampayutte vedanāsaṅkhātamanasikārapaṭilābhenā’’ti.**  
*Because it is to be acquired by this yogi through application of mind, it is called acquisition: "jhānasampayutte vedanāsaṅkhātamanasikārapaṭilābhena" (by the acquisition of the application of mind known as feeling associated with that jhāna).*  

**Assāsapassāsanimittanti assāsapassāse nissāya paṭiladdhapaṭibhāganimittaṃ ārammaṇaṃ kiñcāpi karoti; satiñca sampajaññañca upaṭṭhapetvā pavattanato ārammaṇamukhena tadārammaṇassa paṭisaṃviditattā citte cittānupassīyeva nāmesa hoti.**  
*"Assāsapassāsanimittaṃ" (the sign of the in-and-out breaths): he indeed makes the counterpart sign acquired in dependence on the in-breaths and out-breaths his object; but because his mind occurs having established mindfulness and clear comprehension, it is, through the gateway of the object, "contemplating the mind in the mind" because that object is experienced.*  

**Evaṃ cittānupassanāpi satisampajaññabaleneva hotīti āha ‘‘na hī’’tiādi.**  
*Thus, contemplation of the mind also occurs solely by the power of mindfulness and clear comprehension, which is why he said: "na hi" and so forth.*  

**Pajahati etena, sayaṃ vā pajahatīti pahānaṃ, ñāṇaṃ.**  
*That by which one gives up, or which itself gives up, is "pahānaṃ" (giving up), which is knowledge.*  

**Domanassavasena byāpādanīvaraṇaṃ dassitaṃ tadekaṭṭhabhāvato.**  
*"Domanassavasena byāpādanīvaraṇaṃ dassitaṃ" (by displeasure, the hindrance of ill-will is shown) because they stand in the same category.*  

**Tassāti nīvaraṇapabbassa.**  
*"Tassā" means of the section on hindrances.*  

**Pahānakarañāṇanti pajahanañāṇaṃ.**  
*"Pahānakarañāṇaṃ" is the knowledge that performs the giving up.*  

**Vipassanāparamparanti paṭipāṭiyā vipassanamāha.**  
*"Vipassanāparamparaṃ" (succession of insight): he speaks of insight in succession.*  

**Samathapaṭipannanti majjhimasamathanimittaṃ paṭipannacittaṃ ajjhupekkhati.**  
*"Samathapaṭipannaṃ" means one watches over with equanimity the mind that has entered the sign of intermediate calm.*  

**Ekato upaṭṭhānanti paṭipakkhavigamena ekabhāvena upaṭṭhānaṃ.**  
*"Ekato upaṭṭhānaṃ" means the establishment in a single state by the departure of opposing states.*  

**Sahajātānaṃ ajjhupekkhanā hotīti paggahaniggahasampahaṃsanesu byāpārassa anāpajjitattā ārammaṇānaṃ ajjhupekkhanā, ‘‘yadatthi yaṃ bhūtaṃ taṃ pajahati upekkhaṃ paṭilabhatī’’ti, evaṃ vuttaajjhupekkhanā pavattāti paṭipannā.**  
*"Sahajātānaṃ ajjhupekkhanā hotī" (there is equanimity toward co-nascent states): because of the non-occurrence of effort in exerting, restraining, and gladdening, there is equanimity toward the objects; as it is said, "whatever there is, whatever has become, that he gives up, he acquires equanimity," thus the mentioned watching over with equanimity occurs for the practitioner.*  

**Kevalaṃ nīvaraṇādidhammeti nīvaraṇādidhamme eva pahīne disvā, atha kho tesaṃ pajahanañāṇampi yāthāvato paññāya disvā ajjhupekkhitā hoti.**  
*"Kevalaṃ nīvaraṇādidhamme" (not only the states of hindrances): having seen the hindrances etc. given up, moreover one sees with wisdom that very knowledge of giving up in reality and watches over it with equanimity.*  

**Vuttañhetaṃ bhagavatā ‘‘dhammāpi kho, bhikkhave, pahātabbā, pageva adhammā’’ti (ma. ni. 1.240).**  
*For this is stated by the Blessed One: "Even good states (dhammas) are to be given up, how much more bad states (adhammas)" (Majjhima Nikāya 1.240).*  


### §150

**150. Aniccādivasenapavicinatīti aniccādippakārehi vicinati passati.**  
*150. "Aniccādivasena pavicinati" (investigates by way of impermanence etc.) means he investigates and sees by way of modes such as impermanence.*  

**Nirāmisāti kilesāmisarahitā.**  
*"Nirāmisā" means free of the flesh of defilements.*  

**Kāyikacetasikadarathapaṭippassaddhiyāti kāyacittānaṃ sādhubhāvūpagamanena vikkhambhitattā.**  
*"Kāyikacetasikadarathapaṭippassaddhiyā" (by the calming of bodily and mental distress) means by the suppression of distress through the acquisition of a wholesome state of body and mind.*  

**Sahajātadhammānaṃ ekasabhāvena pavattiyā sahajātaajjhupekkhanāya ajjhupekkhitā hoti.**  
*Because of the occurrence of co-nascent states in a single nature, "sahajātaajjhupekkhanāya ajjhupekkhitā hoti" (watches over closely by way of equanimity toward co-nascent states).*  

**Tasmiṃ kāye pavattā kāyārammaṇā sati, pubbabhāgiyo satisambojjhaṅgo.**  
*The mindfulness occurring in that body, having that body as object, is the preliminary awakening factor of mindfulness.*  

**Esa nayo sesesupi.**  
*Esa nayo sesesupi (this same method applies to the rest).*  

**Somanassasahagatacittuppādavasena cetaṃ okkamanaṃ oliyanaṃ kosajjaṃ, tato ativattanaṃ atidhāvanaṃ uddhaccaṃ, tadubhayavidhurā bojjhaṅgupekkhābhūtā anosakkanaanativattanasaṅkhātā majjhattākāratā.**  
*Because of the arising of mind accompanied by joy, there is a sinking down, a lagging, which is laziness (kosajja); then there is an overrunning, a running too fast, which is restlessness (uddhacca); the state of neutrality, free from both of these, consisting of non-slacking and non-overrunning, is the equanimity factor of awakening.*  

**Idāni yatheva hītiādinā tameva majjhattākāraṃ upamāya vibhāveti.**  
*Now, by "yatheva" etc., he clarifies that very state of neutrality by an analogy.*  

**Tudanaṃ vā patodena.**  
*"Tudanaṃ vā" (whipping) is with a goad.*  

**Ākaḍḍhanaṃ vā rasminā.**  
*"Ākaḍḍhanaṃ vā" (pulling back) is with the reins.*  

**Natthi na kātabbaṃ atthi.**  
*"Natthi" means there is no need to do so.*  

**Ekacittakkhaṇikāti ekekasmiṃ citte vipassanāvasena saha uppajjanakā.**  
*"Ekacittakkhaṇikā" means arising together in each single mind by way of insight.*  

**Nānārasalakkhaṇāti nānākiccā ceva nānāsabhāvā ca.**  
*"Nānārasalakkhaṇā" means having both different functions (kicca) and different individual natures (sabhāva).*  


### §152

**152. Vuttatthāneva sabbāsavasuttavaṇṇanāyaṃ (ma. ni. aṭṭha. 27) ānāpānārammaṇā aparāparaṃ pavattasatiyo ārammaṇasīsena tadārammaṇā dhammā gahitā, tā panekasantāne lokiyacittasampayuttāti lokiyā, tā vaḍḍhamānā lokiyaṃ catubbidhampi satipaṭṭhānaṃ paripūrenti.**  
*152. "Vuttatthāneva" (have the meanings already stated) in the explanation of the Sabbāsava Sutta (Majjhima Nikāya Commentary 27): the repeated mindfulnesses having the in-breaths and out-breaths as object are taken under the heading of the object, and the states having that as object. Since they are associated with mundane minds in a single continuity, they are mundane; when developing, they fulfill the mundane fourfold foundation of mindfulness.*  

**Vijjāvimuttiphalanibbananti vimuttīnaṃ phalabhūtaṃ tehiyeva veditabbaṃ kilesanibbānaṃ, amatamahānibbānameva vijjāvimuttīnaṃ adhigamena adhigantabbatāya tathā vuttaṃ.**  
*"Vijjāvimuttiphalanibbānaṃ" refers to the destruction of defilements which is to be known as the fruit of those liberations, or the deathless great Nibbāna itself, spoken of in this way because it is to be attained by the attainment of knowledge and freedom.*  

**Paripūraṇañcassa ārammaṇaṃ katvā amatassānubhavanameva.**  
*And the fulfillment of it is the experiencing of the deathless by making it the object.*  

**Idha sutte lokiyāpi bojjhaṅgā kathitā lokuttarāpīti ettakaṃ gahetvā, ‘‘iti lokiyassa āgataṭṭhāne lokiyaṃ kathita’’nti ca atthavaṇṇanāvasena aṭṭhakathāyaṃ kathitaṃ.**  
*Taking it that in this sutta both mundane and supramundane factors of awakening are spoken of, "iti lokiyassa āgataṭṭhāne lokiyaṃ kathitaṃ" (thus, where the mundane occurs, the mundane is spoken of) and so forth is stated in the commentary by way of explaining the meaning.*  

**Theroti mahādhammarakkhitatthero.**  
*"Thero" refers to the Elder Mahādhammarakkhita.*  

**Aññattha evaṃ hotīti aññasmiṃ lokiyalokuttaradhammānaṃ tattha tattha vomissakanayena āgatasutte evaṃ lokiyaṃ āgataṃ, idha lokuttaraṃ āgatanti kathetabbaṃ hoti.**  
*"Aññattha evaṃ hoti" (elsewhere it is so): in other suttas where mundane and supramundane states are taught in a mixed way here and there, it should be said "here the mundane occurs, here the supramundane occurs."*  

**Lokuttaraṃ upari āgatanti vijjāvimuttiṃ paripūrentīti evaṃ lokuttaraṃ upari desanāyaṃ āgataṃ; tasmā lokiyā eva bojjhaṅgā vijjāvimutti paripūrikā kathetabbā lokuttarānaṃ bojjhaṅgānaṃ vijjāgahaṇena gahitattā, tasmā therena vuttoyevettha attho gaheto.**  
*"Lokuttaraṃ upari āgata" (the supramundane occurs above): in the phrase "fulfill knowledge and freedom," the supramundane occurs in the teaching above; therefore, the mundane factors of awakening themselves are to be spoken of as fulfilling knowledge and freedom, because the supramundane factors of awakening are grasped by the word "knowledge" (vijjā); therefore, the meaning spoken by the Elder should indeed be accepted here.*  

**Sesaṃ vuttanayattā suviññeyyameva.**  
*The rest is easily understood as it has already been explained.*  

**_Ānāpānassatisuttavaṇṇanāya līnatthappakāsanā samattā._**  
*The explanation of the hidden meaning of the commentary on the Ānāpānasati Sutta is complete.*
