# Pakaraṇa — Post-Canonical Treatises

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Vault Home]]

> [!NOTE]
> *Pakaraṇa* (Pali: "treatise", "independent work") denotes post-canonical scholarly texts that synthesize and systematize the canonical teachings. They are neither part of the Tipiṭaka (Pali Canon) nor commentaries on a specific canonical text, but stand as independent works of doctrinal and practical importance within the Theravāda tradition.
>
> These texts are treated as authoritative within the Mahāvihāra Theravāda tradition but are not the word of the Buddha (*buddhavacana*). Where they conflict with a canonical text, the canon takes precedence.

## Texts in this collection

### [[pakarana/visuddhimagga/INDEX|Visuddhimagga — Path of Purification]]
*Buddhaghosa, 5th century CE*

The Theravāda tradition's most comprehensive meditation manual. Organized around the seven purifications ([[seven_purifications]]), it covers the complete path from virtue through concentration to insight. The samādhi section (Chapters III–XIII) is the fullest systematic treatment of the 40 meditation subjects, the jhānas, and the formless attainments in classical Pali literature.
