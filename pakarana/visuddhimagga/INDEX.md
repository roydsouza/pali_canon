# Visuddhimagga — Path of Purification

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Vault Home]] / [[pakarana/INDEX|Pakaraṇa]]

> [!NOTE]
> *Visuddhimagga* ("Path of Purification") was composed by **Buddhaghosa** at the Mahāvihāra monastery in Sri Lanka, approximately 5th century CE. It is the most comprehensive systematic treatment of Theravāda meditation practice and doctrine. The entire text is organized around the **seven purifications** (*satta visuddhi*) framework drawn from the Rathavinīta Sutta (MN 24).
>
> This vault contains the meditation-relevant chapters. The English content draws on Bhikkhu Nyanamoli's translation as revised by Bhikkhu Bodhi (*The Path of Purification*, BPS/Pariyatti). Use the `[!QUOTE]` callout blocks in each chapter to add direct passages from your copy.

## Structure of the text

The Visuddhimagga has 23 chapters in three books:

### Book I — Sīla (Virtue, Chapters I–II)
Purification of virtue as the indispensable foundation for meditation.

| Ch. | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| I | Sīlakathā | The meaning of virtue; the four kinds of purified virtue |
| II | Dhutaṅgakathā | The 13 ascetic practices (*dhutaṅga*) |

### Book II — Samādhi (Concentration, Chapters III–XIII)
The 40 meditation subjects, the jhānas, and the formless attainments.

| Ch. | Title | Vault file |
|---|---|---|
| III | Kammaṭṭhānagahaṇaniddesā | Taking a meditation subject; the 40 subjects; matching to temperament |
| IV | Paṭhavīkasiṇakathā | Earth kasiṇa; first jhāna described in detail |
| V | Sesadasakasiṇakathā | Remaining 9 kasiṇas |
| VI | Asubhakathā | 10 foulness contemplations (cemetery meditations) |
| VII | Cha-Anussatikathā | 6 recollections + 4 others; body awareness |
| **VIII** | **Ānāpānasatikathā** | **Mindfulness of breathing — the full 16-step practice** | [[pakarana/visuddhimagga/vsm08_anapanasati\|→ Chapter VIII]] |
| IX | Brahmavihārakathā | The four divine abidings; jhāna through loving-kindness |
| X | Arūpakathā | The four formless attainments |
| **XI** | **Samādhikathā** | **Concentration defined; the jhāna factors; 4 jhānas analysed** | [[pakarana/visuddhimagga/vsm11_samadhi\|→ Chapter XI]] |
| XII | Abhiññākathā | The five supernormal powers |
| XIII | Samāpattikathā | The eight attainments and their mastery |

### Book III — Paññā (Wisdom, Chapters XIV–XXIII)
Abhidhamma analysis of mind and matter, the insight knowledges through to liberation.

| Ch. | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| XIV | Khandhāniddesa | The five aggregates |
| XV | Āyatananiddesa | The 12 sense bases |
| XVI | Dhātuniddesa | The 18 elements |
| XVII | Indriyasaccasaniddesā | The faculties, truths, dependent origination |
| XVIII | Diṭṭhivisuddhiniddesa | Purification of view (1st insight knowledge) |
| XIX | Kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhikathā | Purification by overcoming doubt |
| XX | Maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhikathā | Knowledge of what is and is not the path |
| XXI | Paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhikathā | 9 insight knowledges: arising/passing, dissolution, fear, misery, disenchantment, desire for deliverance, reflection, equanimity |
| XXII | Ñāṇadassanavisuddhikathā | Path and fruition; nibbāna |
| XXIII | Paññābhāvanānisaṃsakathā | Benefits of developing wisdom |

## Reading guidance

The Visuddhimagga is dense and technical. For a meditation practitioner it is best read **selectively and slowly** alongside the relevant canonical suttas, not sequentially. Suggested approach:

1. **Start with Chapter VIII** if ānāpānasati is your primary sitting practice — it directly extends MN 118
2. **Read Chapter XI** when the jhānas become an active part of your practice — it gives the most precise Pali definition of each jhāna factor
3. **Come to Chapters XVIII–XXII** only after samatha practice is established — the insight knowledge sequence assumes a meditator with access concentration at minimum
