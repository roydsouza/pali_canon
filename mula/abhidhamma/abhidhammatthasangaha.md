# Abhidhammatthasaṅgaha (A Comprehensive Manual of Abhidhamma)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[mula/INDEX|Mūla]] / [[mula/abhidhamma/INDEX|Abhidhamma Piṭaka]]

*Written by Ācariya Anuruddha (circa 11th–12th century).*

---

## Overview

The **Abhidhammatthasaṅgaha** is the foundational compendium of the Abhidhamma Piṭaka in the Theravāda tradition. It provides a systematic summary of the ultimate realities (*paramattha dhamma*) analyzed across the seven books of the Abhidhamma.

According to this compendium, there are four ultimate realities:
1. **Consciousness (Citta)**
2. **Mental Factors (Cetasika)**
3. **Material Phenomena (Rūpa)**
4. **The Unconditioned Element (Nibbāna)**

---

## The Nine Chapters (Nava Paricchedā)

### 1. Citta-saṅgaha (Compendium of Consciousness)
*   **Pali Title**: *Cittasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Classifies consciousness into 89 (or 121) types based on planes of existence (*bhūmi*) and ethical qualities (*jāti*):
    *   **Sensuous plane consciousness (Kāmāvacara citta)**: 54 types (wholesome, unwholesome, resultant, functional).
    *   **Fine-material sphere consciousness (Rūpāvacara citta)**: 15 types associated with the [[matika/four_jhanas|Four Jhānas]].
    *   **Immaterial sphere consciousness (Arūpāvacara citta)**: 12 types associated with formless attainments.
    *   **Supramundane consciousness (Lokuttara citta)**: 8 (or 40) types associated with the stages of awakening.

### 2. Cetasika-saṅgaha (Compendium of Mental Factors)
*   **Pali Title**: *Cetasikasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Details the 52 mental factors that arise and cease together with consciousness, sharing its object and base:
    *   **Ethically variable factors (Aññasamāna)**: 13 factors (7 universal, 6 occasional).
    *   **Unwholesome factors (Akusala)**: 14 factors including greed (*lobha*), hatred (*dosa*), and delusion (*moha*), linked to the [[matika/three_unwholesome_roots|Three Unwholesome Roots]].
    *   **Beautiful factors (Sobhana)**: 25 factors including mindfulness (*sati*), non-greed (*alobha*), and non-hatred (*adosa*).

### 3. Pakiṇṇaka-saṅgaha (Compendium of Miscellaneous Topics)
*   **Pali Title**: *Pakiṇṇakasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Groups consciousness and mental factors according to six miscellaneous categories:
    *   **Feeling (Vedanā)**: Pleasure, pain, joy, displeasure, equanimity.
    *   **Root (Hetu)**: The six roots (wholesome and unwholesome).
    *   **Function (Kicca)**: The 14 functions of consciousness (e.g., rebirth-linking, sub-consciousness, receiving, investigating, cognitive active phase).
    *   **Door (Dvāra)**: The six sense-doors.
    *   **Object (Ārammaṇa)**: The six types of objects.
    *   **Base (Vatthu)**: The physical bases of consciousness.

### 4. Vīthi-saṅgaha (Compendium of Cognitive Process)
*   **Pali Title**: *Vīthisaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Systematizes the sequence of consciousness moments (*cittakkhana*) occurring in cognitive processes through the five sense-doors (*pañcadvāravīthi*) and the mind-door (*manodvāravīthi*). Details the active phase of consciousness (*javana*) where karma is generated.

### 5. Vīthimutta-saṅgaha (Compendium of Cognitive Process-Freed)
*   **Pali Title**: *Vīthimuttasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Analyzes subjects outside the active cognitive process:
    *   **Planes of existence (Bhūmi)**: The 31 planes of rebirth.
    *   **Karma (Kamma)**: Classifications of karma by function, order of ripening, time of ripening, and place of ripening.
    *   **Death and Rebirth (Maraṇuppatti)**: The process of transition from death-consciousness (*cuti citta*) to rebirth-linking consciousness (*paṭisandhi citta*).

### 6. Rūpa-saṅgaha (Compendium of Matter & Nibbāna)
*   **Pali Title**: *Rūpasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Classifies material phenomena and defines the unconditioned:
    *   **Matter (Rūpa)**: The 28 material elements (4 primary elements/[[matika/form_or_matter|mahābhūta]] and 24 derived elements/*upādārūpa*).
    *   **Nibbāna**: The cessation of suffering, experienced as the unconditioned element (*asaṅkhatā dhātu*) with the qualities of peace, security, and liberation from rebirth.

### 7. Samuccaya-saṅgaha (Compendium of Categories)
*   **Pali Title**: *Samuccayasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Groups ultimate realities into traditional doctrinal lists matching the Sutta teachings:
    *   **Unwholesome categories**: Taints (*āsava*), floods (*ogha*), yokes (*yoga*), [[matika/ten_fetters|Ten Fetters (Saṃyojana)]], and [[matika/five_hindrances|Five Hindrances (Nīvaraṇa)]].
    *   **Awakening factors (Bodhipakkhiyadhamma)**: Satipaṭṭhāna, Sammappadhāna, Iddhipāda, Indriya, Bala, [[matika/seven_awakening_factors|Seven Awakening Factors (Bojjhaṅga)]], and the [[matika/noble_eightfold_path|Noble Eightfold Path]].

### 8. Paccaya-saṅgaha (Compendium of Conditioning)
*   **Pali Title**: *Paccayasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Explains the relations of conditioning that govern all conditioned phenomena:
    *   **Dependent Origination (Paṭiccasamuppāda)**: The twelve-linked chain of conditioning. (See [[matika/dependent_origination|Dependent Origination]]).
    *   **The Patthana Method (Patthānānaya)**: The 24 conditional relations (e.g., root condition, object condition, proximity condition, support condition).

### 9. Kammaṭṭhāna-saṅgaha (Compendium of Meditation Subjects)
*   **Pali Title**: *Kammaṭṭhānasaṅgahavibhāga*
*   **Analysis**: Systematizes meditation practice into two systems:
    *   **Tranquility Meditation (Samatha)**: The 40 traditional meditation subjects (kasinas, recollections, sublime states, formless states) leading to the [[matika/four_jhanas|Four Jhānas]].
    *   **Insight Meditation (Vipassanā)**: The stages of purification (*visuddhi*) and insight knowledges (*vipassanā-ñāṇa*) leading to liberation. (See [[matika/seven_purifications|Seven Purifications]]).

---

## Doctrinal Integration
The Abhidhammatthasaṅgaha serves as the ultimate map of the mind and matter, classifying the realities experienced during meditation. In practice:
*   The **five aggregates** are mapped to Citta (consciousness aggregate), Cetasika (feeling, perception, mental formations aggregates), and Rūpa (material form aggregate).
*   The **noble truths** are realized through the progression of insight outlined in Chapter 9.
