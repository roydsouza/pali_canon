# Vinaya Piṭaka — Mūla

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]] / [[mula/INDEX|Mūla]]

The Vinaya Piṭaka ("Discipline Basket") contains the rules and regulations for the monastic Saṅgha, and is the primary canonical source for the Buddhist precepts.

## Detailed Structure of the Vinaya Piṭaka

### 1. Suttavibhaṅga (Rule Analysis)
The Suttavibhaṅga provides the Pātimokkha rules alongside the origin stories (*nidāna*) explaining why each rule was laid down, a word-by-word commentary (*padabhājanīya*), and exceptions (*anāpatti*).
*   **Mahāvibhaṅga**: The commentary and analysis of the 227 rules for monks (Bhikkhus). See [[patimokkha_bhikkhu|Bhikkhu Pātimokkha]].
*   **Bhikkhunīvibhaṅga**: The commentary and analysis of the 311 rules for nuns (Bhikkhunīs). See [[patimokkha_bhikkhuni|Bhikkhunī Pātimokkha]].

### 2. Khandhaka (The Divisions)
The Khandhaka organizes monastic regulations and community procedures into 22 chapters (*khandhakas*), split into two volumes:

#### Mahāvagga (The Great Division)
1.  **Mahākhandhaka**: Admission and ordination procedures (*pabbajjā* and *upasampadā*).
2.  **Uposathakhandhaka**: The fortnightly uposatha ceremony and Pātimokkha recitation.
3.  **Vassūpanāyikakhandhaka**: Guidelines for entering the rains retreat (*vassa*).
4.  **Pavāraṇākhandhaka**: The pavāraṇā ceremony marking the end of the rains retreat.
5.  **Cammakkhandhaka**: Rules regarding footwear, leather items, and vehicles.
6.  **Bhesajjakkhandhaka**: Medicines and allowable foods for monastics.
7.  **Kathinakkhandhaka**: The kathina robe-offering ceremony.
8.  **Cīvarakkhandhaka**: Rules regarding monastic robes, materials, and distribution.
9.  **Campeyyakkhandhaka**: Valid and invalid formal acts of the Sangha (*saṅghakamma*).
10. **Kosambakakkhandhaka**: Settlement of internal disputes (based on the Kosambī schism).

#### Cūḷavagga (The Lesser Division)
1.  **Kammakkhandhaka**: Penal procedures and disciplinary measures.
2.  **Pārivāsikakkhandhaka**: Conduct rules for monks undergoing probation (*parivāsa*).
3.  **Samuccayakkhandhaka**: Procedures for resolving multiple or concealed offenses.
4.  **Samathakkhandhaka**: Methods for settling disputes (*adhikaraṇasamatha*).
5.  **Khuddakavatthukkhandhaka**: Daily life rules (hygiene, robes, personal items).
6.  **Senāsanakkhandhaka**: Lodging, monastery buildings, and furniture.
7.  **Saṅghabhedakakkhandhaka**: Schism and the actions of Devadatta.
8.  **Vattakkhandhaka**: Monastic duties and protocols between seniors, juniors, and visitors.
9.  **Pātimokkhatthapanakhandhaka**: Rules for suspending the Pātimokkha recitation.
10. **Bhikkhunīkhandhaka**: Ordination and specific community rules for nuns.
11. **Pañcasatikakkhandhaka**: History of the First Council at Rājagaha.
12. **Sattasatikakkhandhaka**: History of the Second Council at Vesālī.

### 3. Parivāra (The Accessory)
The Parivāra is a later handbook containing catechisms, summaries, and classifications of the rules:
*   **Soḷasamahāvāra**: The sixteen chapters analyzing rules by origin, offense, and mitigation.
*   **Samuṭṭhānasīsasaṅgaha**: The combinations of body, speech, and mind that generate offenses.
*   **Gāthāsaṅgaha**: Summary verses for teaching.
*   **Adhikaraṇabheda**: Categorization of monastic legal disputes.
