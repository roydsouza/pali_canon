The Bhikkhu Pātimokkha is the core code of monastic discipline containing 227 rules. These rules are divided into eight sections based on the severity of the offense and the corresponding disciplinary action.

---

## 1. Pārājika (Defeat — 4 Rules)
An offense that results in immediate and permanent expulsion from the Sangha.

1. **Sexual Intercourse (Methunadhamma)**  
   *Engaging in sexual intercourse.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #3 (Sexual Misconduct)]] (elevated to complete celibacy / *abrahmacariya* in monastic code).
2. **Stealing (Adinnādāna)**  
   *Taking what is not given (value of five māsakas or more).*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #2 (Abstaining from stealing)]].
3. **Taking Human Life (Manussaviggaha)**  
   *Intentionally depriving a human being of life or encouraging suicide.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #1 (Abstaining from killing)]].
4. **Falsely Claiming Spiritual Attainments (Uttarimanussadhamma)**  
   *Lying about possessing jhānas, psychic powers, or path attainments.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #4 (Abstaining from false speech)]].

---

## 2. Saṅghādisesa (Formal Meeting of the Community — 13 Rules)
Serious offenses requiring suspension, probation (*parivāsa*), and reinstatement by a assembly of at least 20 monks.
- **Rules 1–5**: Sexual misconduct (semen release, physical contact, suggestive speech, proposing sex, matchmaking).
- **Rules 6–7**: Monastic dwelling construction regulations.
- **Rules 8–9**: Groundless accusations of pārājika.
- **Rules 10–11**: Promoting schism within the Sangha.
- **Rules 12–13**: Obstinacy and corrupting lay families.

---

## 3. Aniyata (Indefinite — 2 Rules)
Cases where a monk is accused of being alone with a woman, and the offense (Pārājika, Saṅghādisesa, or Pācittiya) is judged based on witness testimony.

---

## 4. Nissaggiya Pācittiya (Forfeiture and Confession — 30 Rules)
Rules governing the possession of robes, bowls, rugs, and money. The item must be forfeited and the offense confessed.
- **Rules 1–10**: Robe-season regulations.
- **Rules 18–19**: Prohibiting acceptance of gold, silver, or money.
- **Rules 21–22**: Regulations on spare bowls.

---

## 5. Pācittiya (Confession — 92 Rules)
Minor offenses that must be acknowledged and confessed to another monk.
- **Rule 1 (Lying)**: Abstaining from deliberate lies. (*Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #4]]).
- **Rule 10 (Digging)**: Digging soil or cutting vegetation.
- **Rule 51 (Intoxicants)**: Drinking alcohol or fermented liquors. (*Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #5]]).
- **Rule 61 (Killing Animals)**: Intentionally depriving an animal of life. (*Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #1]]).

---

## 6. Pāṭidesanīya (To Be Confessed — 4 Rules)
Offenses regarding receiving food from uninvited nuns or in certain situations, which must be formally acknowledged.

---

## 7. Sekhiya (Training Rules — 75 Rules)
Etiquette and behavior rules for eating, walking, and teaching Dhamma.

---

## 8. Adhikaraṇasamatha (Settlement of Disputes — 7 Rules)
Procedures for resolving disputes and legal issues within the Sangha.
