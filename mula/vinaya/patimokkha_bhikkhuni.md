The Bhikkhunī Pātimokkha is the core code of discipline for Buddhist nuns containing 311 rules. These rules are divided into seven sections based on the severity of the offense.

---

## 1. Pārājika (Offenses Entailing Defeat — 8 Rules)
Offenses that result in immediate expulsion from the Sangha.
1. **Sexual Intercourse (Methunadhamma)**  
   *Engaging in sexual intercourse.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #3]] (complete celibacy).
2. **Stealing (Adinnādāna)**  
   *Taking what is not given.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #2]].
3. **Taking Human Life (Manussaviggaha)**  
   *Depriving a human of life.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #1]].
4. **Claiming Attainments (Uttarimanussadhamma)**  
   *Lying about spiritual attainments.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #4]].
5. **Physical Contact (Ubbhajāṇumaṇḍalika)**  
   *Consenting to physical contact with a man below the collarbone and above the knee.*  
   *Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #3]] (monastic boundary).
6. **Concealing Offenses (Vajjasacchādana)**  
   *Concealing another nun's pārājika offense.*
7. **Following a Suspended Monk (Ukkhipitānuvattikā)**  
   *Reverently following a monk suspended by the Sangha.*
8. **Consenting to Touch (Aṭṭhavatthuka)**  
   *Consenting to a man touching or taking hold of hand, robe, or body.*

---

## 2. Saṅghādisesa (Offenses Entailing Formal Meetings — 17 Rules)
Serious offenses requiring suspension, probation, and reinstatement.
- **Rules 1–4**: Monastic litigation and admitting outcasts.
- **Rule 5**: Shaving the head without permission.
- **Rules 8–9**: Groundless accusations.
- **Rules 13–17**: Disobedience and schismatic actions.

---

## 3. Nissaggiya Pācittiya (Offenses Entailing Forfeiture and Confession — 30 Rules)
Offenses regarding the possession of robes, bowls, rugs, and gold/silver.
- **Rules 1–10**: Robes and bowls storage limits.
- **Rules 11–12**: Buying or exchanging items.
- **Rule 19**: Accepting gold, silver, or money. (*Related Precept*: [[matika/eight_precepts|Eight Precepts #8]] / monastic restraint).

---

## 4. Pācittiya (Offenses Entailing Confession — 166 Rules)
Minor offenses that must be acknowledged and confessed.
- **Rule 1 (Lying)**: Deliberate lies. (*Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #4]]).
- **Rule 10 (Digging)**: Digging soil or damaging trees.
- **Rule 51 (Intoxicants)**: Drinking alcohol. (*Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #5]]).
- **Rule 61 (Killing Animals)**: Depriving an animal of life. (*Related Precept*: [[matika/five_precepts|Five Precepts #1]]).

---

## 5. Pāṭidesanīya (Offenses Entailing Acknowledgement — 8 Rules)
Offenses regarding accepting food under inappropriate conditions.

---

## 6. Sekhiya (Training Rules — 75 Rules)
Monastic training on manners, posture, eating, and teaching Dhamma (shared with Bhikkhus).

---

## 7. Adhikaraṇasamatha (Settling Disputes — 7 Rules)
monastic procedures for resolving disputes and legal cases within the Sangha.
