# Simile Index (Upamā-saṅgaha)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]]

This index compiles major similes (*upamā*) used by the Buddha and his senior disciples to illustrate key aspects of the Dhamma.

---

## 🌊 Practice & Path

### 1. The Raft Simile (*Kollūpama*)
*   **Source**: [[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn22|MN 22: Alagaddūpamasutta]]
*   **Description**: A traveler builds a raft of grass, twigs, and branches to cross a great river. Having crossed, they do not carry the raft on their head; they leave it behind.
*   **Meaning**: The Dhamma is for crossing over (*nittharaṇattha*), not for holding onto (*gahaṇattha*). Even wholesome states must eventually be let go, let alone unwholesome ones.

### 2. The Lute Strings (*Vīṇūpama*)
*   **Source**: [[mula/sutta/anguttara_nikaya/INDEX|AN 6.55: Soṇasutta]]
*   **Description**: A lute cannot play a beautiful tune if the strings are stretched too tight or left too loose. It only plays well when tuned in balance.
*   **Meaning**: Energy (*viriya*) must be balanced with calmness. Too much energy leads to restlessness; too little leads to laziness.

### 3. The Six Animals (*Chappāṇaka*)
*   **Source**: [[mula/sutta/samyutta_nikaya/INDEX|SN 35.247: Chappāṇakasutta]]
*   **Description**: Six different animals (dog, jackal, crocodile, bird, hyena, monkey) are tied to a single post with ropes. Each tries to pull towards its own habitat. They only rest when they are exhausted, or when the post is firm.
*   **Meaning**: The six sense bases pull the mind toward their objects. Mindfulness of the body (*kāyagatāsati*) is the firm post (*thambha*) that anchors the senses and prevents them from wandering.

## 🧩 The Five Aggregates

### 1. The Five Natural Metaphors
*   **Source**: [[mula/sutta/samyutta_nikaya/INDEX|SN 22.95: Phenapiṇḍūpamasutta]]
*   **Description**:
    *   **Material Form (Rūpa)** is compared to a **lump of foam** floating down the Ganges.
    *   **Feeling (Vedanā)** is compared to a **water bubble** in rain.
    *   **Perception (Saññā)** is compared to a **mirage** in the heat of midday.
    *   **Volitional Formations (Saṅkhārā)** are compared to a **plantain trunk** (which has no heartwood).
    *   **Consciousness (Viññāṇa)** is compared to a **conjurer's trick/illusion**.
*   **Meaning**: Illustrates the emptiness (*suññatā*), insubstantiality, and lack of core self in the five aggregates.

### 2. The Sheaves of Reeds (*Naḷakalāpī*)
*   **Source**: [[mula/sutta/samyutta_nikaya/INDEX|SN 12.67: Naḷakalāpīsutta]]
*   **Description**: Two sheaves of reeds lean against each other. If one sheave is pulled away, the other falls down.
*   **Meaning**: Illustrates reciprocal conditioning in dependent origination. Consciousness (*viññāṇa*) and name-and-form (*nāmarūpa*) condition each other; one cannot exist without the other in a living being.

### 3. The Elephant's Footprint (*Hatthipadopama*)
*   **Source**: [[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn28|MN 28: Mahāhatthipadopamasutta]]
*   **Description**: The footprint of any walking animal easily fits inside the massive footprint of an elephant.
*   **Meaning**: All wholesome qualities, including the five aggregates, fit within the framework of the Four Noble Truths.

## 🍳 Awakening & Freedom

### 1. Vegetables Boiled Dry in a Pot (*Sukkhaḍāka*)
*   **Source**: [[mula/sutta/khuddaka_nikaya/therigatha/thig1_1|THIG 1.1: Aññatarātherīgāthā]]
*   **Description**: Leafy vegetables cooked in a pot with water are boiled dry until all the water is gone and no steam remains.
*   **Meaning**: Represents the complete eradication of desire and latent defilements through the fire of wisdom, leaving no trace for future rebirth. (See commentary [[atthakatha/sutta/khuddaka_nikaya/therigatha/thig1_1_att|THIG 1.1 Commentary]]).
