# Pericope Concordance (Vākya-saṅgaha)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]]

This concordance maps the recurrent stock formulas (pericopes) used in the Pali suttas to explain core doctrines and meditative attainments.

---

## 🧘 Meditative Attainments

### 1. The Four Jhānas Formula
*   **Setting**: Settings of meditation attainment.
*   **Pali Text**:
    > *“Vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati...”*
*   **Description**:
    *   **First Jhāna**: Accompanied by directed thought and evaluation, and the rapture and pleasure born of seclusion.
    *   **Second Jhāna**: Free from directed thought and evaluation, with internal confidence and unification of mind, and the rapture and pleasure born of concentration.
    *   **Third Jhāna**: With the fading of rapture, remaining equanimous, mindful, and alert, experiencing pleasure with the body.
    *   **Fourth Jhāna**: Neither pleasure nor pain, with the purification of mindfulness by equanimity.
*   **Loci**:
    *   [[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn10|MN 10: Satipaṭṭhānasutta]]
    *   [[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn118|MN 118: Ānāpānasatisutta]]
    *   [[matika/four_jhanas|Mātikā: Four Jhānas]]

## 🧩 Insight & Contemplation

### 1. Aggregates Liberation Formula (*Anattalakkhaṇa*)
*   **Setting**: Discourses on insight into the three marks.
*   **Pali Text**:
    > *“N'etaṃ mama, n'esohamasmi, na meso attā.”*  
    > *(“This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.”)*
*   **Meaning**: Used to systematically dismantle the illusion of identity and ownership over the five aggregates.
*   **Loci**:
    *   [[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn28|MN 28: Mahāhatthipadopamasutta]]
    *   [[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn44|MN 44: Cūḷavedallasutta]]
    *   [[matika/five_aggregates|Mātikā: Five Aggregates]]

### 2. Element Dissection Formula
*   **Setting**: Mindfulness of body element analysis.
*   **Pali Text**:
    > *“Kāyaṃyeva yathābhūtaṃ yathāṭhitaṃ dhātuso paccavekkhati: ‘Atthi imasmiṃ kāye pathavīdhātu āpodhātu tejodhātu vāyodhātū’ti.”*  
    > *(“One reviews this very body, however it stands or is positioned, by way of elements: ‘In this body there is the earth element, the water element, the fire element, the wind element.’”)*
*   **Loci**:
    *   [[mula/sutta/majjhima_nikaya/mn10|MN 10: Satipaṭṭhānasutta §4.1]]
    *   [[matika/contemplation_of_body|Mātikā: Contemplation of the Body]]
    *   [[matika/form_or_matter|Mātikā: Form or Matter]]

## 🏆 Spiritual Fruits

### 1. The Declaration of Arhathood
*   **Setting**: The standard ending of a disciple's discourse when they attain liberation.
*   **Pali Text**:
    > *“Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātīti.”*  
    > *(“Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being.”)*
