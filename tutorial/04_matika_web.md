---

## What the mātikā web is

*Mātikā* (Pali: "matrix" or "list") refers to the canonical doctrinal lists — the numbered groupings that structure the Buddha's teaching: the Four Noble Truths, the Noble Eightfold Path, the Five Aggregates, the Seven Awakening Factors, and so on. There are 22 such lists in this vault, each with its own index note.

The web is **bidirectional**: every mātikā note links forward to all suttas that treat that doctrine, and every sutta links back to all mātikā lists it touches. This creates a doctrinal navigation layer on top of the chronological/nikāya layer.

Start at [[matika/INDEX|Mātikā Index]] to see all 22 lists.

---

## A worked example: following *bojjhaṅga*

Suppose you want to understand the seven awakening factors (*satta bojjhaṅgā*) across the canon.

**Step 1:** Open [[seven_awakening_factors]]. You will see:
- A Pali definition of each factor
- An English description with practice notes
- Links to every vault sutta that mentions the awakening factors

**Step 2:** Notice that the awakening factors appear in:
- MN 118 (ānāpānasati → awakening factors)
- SN 46 (the full Bojjhaṅgasaṃyutta — 56 suttas)
- MN 10/DN 22 (fourth foundation, dhammānupassanā)
- AN 7.65 (factors of heedfulness)
- Dhammapada vaggas that touch on the liberated mind

Each entry is a direct wikilink. Click through to see how the *same* factors appear in completely different teaching contexts — in breath meditation, in sense-restraint, in the dying moment.

**Step 3:** Go deeper with SN 46. Open [[sn46|SN 46]] and scan the first few suttas. The Bojjhaṅgasaṃyutta is the canonical home of the awakening factors — 56 suttas devoted exclusively to them. The commentary ([[sn46_att|SN 46 Commentary]]) explains the situational application: some factors are cultivated when the mind is sluggish; others when it is agitated.

This is the mātikā web in use: you entered at a list, traced a theme across three nikāyas, and arrived at its deepest canonical treatment — all within the vault.

## Using the web during practice

The mātikā web has a direct practice use. When a factor arises in sitting — say, *pīti* (rapture) appears — you can:

1. Open [[seven_awakening_factors]] → find the *pīti* entry
2. See which suttas describe it, how to cultivate it, and when not to over-cultivate it
3. Open the relevant SN 46 sutta for the canonical instruction

This is not distraction — it is bringing canonical precision to bear on a live experience. Conversely, if a hindrance arises and you want to know its antidote, open [[five_hindrances]] and follow its cross-links to SN 46 and the [[paths/working_with_hindrances|Working with the Hindrances path]].

## Creating your own doctrinal threads

The mātikā notes are also starting points for your own study threads. Some examples:

**"How does craving work?"** Start at [[dependent_origination]] → follow to SN 12 → follow to MN 44 (precise definition of craving) → follow to [[understanding_craving|the craving path]].

**"What are the hindrances to my sitting?"** Start at [[five_hindrances]] → [[paths/working_with_hindrances|Working with Hindrances path]] → SN 46 (antidotes).

**"I keep hearing about *vipassanā* — what does the canon actually say?"** Start at [[four_foundations_of_mindfulness]] → MN 10 → [[paths/vipassana_practice|Vipassanā path]] → MN 148, MN 28, MN 2.
