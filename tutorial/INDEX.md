# Dhamma Practice Workstation

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Vault Home]]

> [!NOTE]
> This is a personal Obsidian vault containing the Pali Canon in its original language alongside classical commentaries, a doctrinal cross-reference web, NLP reading aids, and guided practice paths. Everything here supports one goal: developing a serious meditation practice rooted in the Theravāda canonical tradition.

---

## Where to begin

If you are here for **meditation practice**, start with the breath:

> **→ [[tutorial/01_breath_practice|Tutorial 1: Sitting with the Ānāpānasati Sutta]]**

This single sutta — the Buddha's most systematic teaching on breath meditation — is the natural entry point. The first tutorial takes you from opening the text to understanding how to use it in actual sitting practice.

## How texts are formatted

Every mūla sutta uses **interleaved Pali/English** formatting:

**Evaṁ me sutaṁ—**
*So I have heard.*

**Ekaṁ samayaṁ bhagavā**
*At one time the Buddha*

Bold lines are Pali (Mahāsaṅgīti edition). Italic lines are Bhikkhu Sujato's translation (CC0). You can hide the English at any time with **Cmd+R** to read the Pali alone — useful for recitation and for testing your comprehension.

## A note on how to use these tutorials

Each tutorial is anchored to a specific sutta or vault feature. You will get the most out of them by having the relevant sutta open in a second pane alongside the tutorial. Obsidian's split-pane view (`Cmd+\`) is ideal for this.

The tutorials assume you have Obsidian open with this vault loaded, the Dataview plugin active, and the Simsapa DPD plugin installed. If any of these are missing, see [[meta/SIMSAPA-DPD|Simsapa Setup]].
