# Paritta Collection (Protective Chanting)

**Navigation**: [[INDEX|Pali Canon Vault]]

> [!NOTE]
> *Paritta* refers to the Theravāda Buddhist practice of reciting specific canonical discourses to ward off danger, illness, and evil influences, and to cultivate protective mental states of loving-kindness, truth, and ethical purity.

All protective chants are anchored in the **[[matika/three_refuges|Three Refuges (Tisarana)]]**, as refuge in the Triple Gem is the ultimate protection in Buddhist practice.

## The Power of Truth (Saccakiriyā)
Most parittas operate through the *Saccakiriyā*—an assertion of an undeniable truth of Dhamma (e.g., "By this truth, may there be safety/well-being!"). In the *Ratana Sutta*, each verse ends with:
> *Etena saccena suvatthi hotu.*
> **By this truth, may there be safety.**

In the *Ahi Sutta*, protection is generated by projecting loving-kindness:
> *Virūpakkhehi me mettaṃ, mettaṃ Erāpathehi me...*
> **I have love for the Virūpakkhas, love for the Erāpathas...**
