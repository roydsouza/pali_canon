## 1. Aññatarātherīgāthāvaṇṇanā (Commentary on the Anonymous Nun's Verse)

### Background Story (Atthuppatti)

The commentary records that this verse was spoken by an anonymous nun who went forth out of great faith in the Buddha's dispensation. Before ordaining, she was a laywoman of quiet devotion.

After her ordination, she led a simple life of intense contemplation, dedicating herself to the practice of the four foundations of mindfulness. One night, after sewing her own simple robe out of discarded scraps of cloth (*coḷa*), she sat down to meditate. During her meditation, she attained complete liberation (*arahatta*). As she lay down to rest in the peace of Nibbāna, she joyfully spoke this verse to herself, celebrating the absolute quelling of her desires.

---

### Word-by-Word Commentary

*   **Sukhaṁ supāhi therike** (*“Sleep softly, little nun”*): An affectionate self-address. "Therike" means "little senior nun." "Sleep softly" refers to the peaceful rest of an Arahant, whose sleep is free from disturbing dreams, fear, or mental disturbances.
*   **Katvā coḷena pārutā** (*“Wrapped in the cloth you sewed yourself”*): Literally, she is wrapped in a patchwork robe sewn from discarded rags (*coḷa*). It represents her contentment with the simplest requisites (*cīvara-santuṭṭhi*).
*   **Upasanto hi te rāgo** (*“For your desire has been quelled”*): This is the cause of her peaceful sleep. The fire of lust (*rāgaggi*) has been completely extinguished.
*   **Sukkhaḍākaṁ va kumbhiyaṁ** (*“Like vegetables boiled dry in a pot”*): A vivid simile. Just as leafy vegetables (*ḍāka*) cooked in a pot (*kumbhi*) with water are eventually dried up (*sukkha*) when all the water boils away, leaving nothing left to cook, so too have her defilements and latent tendencies (*anusaya*) been boiled dry by the heat of her energy (*ātāpa*) and wisdom, leaving no karmic seed for future rebirth.

---
