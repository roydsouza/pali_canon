## 1. Subhūtittheragāthāvaṇṇanā (Commentary on Subhūti's Verse)

### Background Story (Atthuppatti)

The commentary explains that Venerable Subhūti was the younger brother of Anāthapiṇḍika. On the day of the dedication of Jetavana Monastery, he heard the Buddha teach and was filled with faith, leading him to go forth into monastic life.

After receiving his ordination, he mastered the two categories of rules and went to reside in the forest to practice meditation. He developed loving-kindness meditation (*mettā-kammatthāna*) as his basis for insight (*vipassanā*). While meditating in the forest, he attained Arhathood. When he returned, he was praised by the Buddha as the foremost of those who dwell in peace (*araṇavihārī*) and those who are worthy of offerings (*dakkhiṇeyyānam*).

When he returned to the forest, the rain deity Pajjunna, in reverence to the elder's spiritual power, ceased the rain over his dwelling to protect him. However, because Subhūti had no proper shelter, the lay devotees built a leaf-hut for him. Once the hut was completed, roofed, and pleasant, the elder sat inside it. Knowing that his mind was completely composed and liberated, he addressed the rain deity: *"Channā me kuṭikā sukhā nivātā, vassa deva yathāsukhaṃ..."* ("My hut is roofed, pleasant, and sheltered... so rain, heavens!").

---

### Word-by-Word Commentary

*   **Channā me kuṭikā** (*“My hut is roofed/covered”*): Literally, the leaf-hut built by the lay devotees is thatched and protected. Metaphorically, it refers to the elder's moral discipline (*sīla*), which is well-roofed and protected against the rain of defilements (*kilesa-vassa*).
*   **Sukhā nivātā** (*“Pleasant, sheltered from the wind”*): Literally, the hut is comfortable and has no draft. Metaphorically, his life is comfortable (*sukha*) because his mind is free from the draft of lust (*rāga*), hatred (*dosa*), and delusion (*moha*).
*   **Vassa deva yathāsukhaṃ** (*“So rain, deva, as you please!”*): This is an invitation to Pajjunna (the rain deity). Since the outer shelter is secure and the inner mind is protected, physical rain cannot disturb his peace.
*   **Cittaṁ me susamāhitaṃ** (*“My mind is serene/well-composed”*): This indicates the mastery of concentration (*adhicitta*), specifically his absorption in loving-kindness (*mettā-jhāna*) and fruit attainment (*phalasamāpatti*).
*   **Vimuttaṃ** (*“Freed”*): His mind is completely released from all defilements and attachments through the realization of Nibbāna (*arahatta-phala*).
*   **Ātāpī viharāmi** (*“I practice/dwell ardently”*): Signifies the presence of energetic effort (*viriya*) and wisdom (*paññā*) that burns away defilements.
